Usage and Examples

Let's explore some common uses of the passive form in Japanese with examples:

1. Describing Experiences

The passive is often used to describe experiences that happen to the subject, especially in formal or written contexts.

私は先生に誉められました。

Watashi wa sensei ni homeraremashita.

I was praised by the teacher.

彼は会社に採用されました。

Kare wa kaisha ni saiyō saremashita.

He was hired by the company.

この映画は多くの人に愛されています。

Kono eiga wa ōku no hito ni aisarete imasu.

This movie is loved by many people.

2. Expressing Inconvenience or Annoyance

The indirect and suffering passive forms are often used to express that the subject was inconvenienced or annoyed by someone's action.

昨日、隣の人にうるさい音楽を聞かれました。

Kinō, tonari no hito ni urusai ongaku o kikaremashita.

Yesterday, my neighbor played loud music (and it bothered me).

子供に夜中に起こされました。

Kodomo ni yonaka ni okosaremashita.

I was woken up by my child in the middle of the night.

友達に約束を破られました。

Tomodachi ni yakusoku o yaburaremashita.

My friend broke their promise to me (and I was affected by it).

3. Describing Things Happening to Possessions

The suffering passive is often used to describe things happening to one's possessions or people close to them.

私は財布を盗まれました。

Watashi wa saifu o nusumaremashita.

I had my wallet stolen.

彼女は弟に日記を読まれました。

Kanojo wa otōto ni nikki o yomaremashita.

She had her diary read by her younger brother.

私は友達に秘密を話されました。

Watashi wa tomodachi ni himitsu o hanasaremashita.

My friend told my secret (and it affected me negatively).

4. Formal Writing and News

The passive form is commonly used in formal writing, academic papers, and news reports, similar to how the passive voice is used in English formal writing.

この法律は2020年に制定されました。

Kono hōritsu wa 2020-nen ni seitei saremashita.

This law was enacted in 2020.

この研究は多くの科学者によって支持されています。

Kono kenkyū wa ōku no kagakusha ni yotte shiji sarete imasu.

This research is supported by many scientists.

昨日、新しい首相が選ばれました。

Kinō, atarashii shushō ga erabaremashita.

Yesterday, a new prime minister was elected.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Transform Verbs into Passive Form

Transform the following verbs into their passive form.

1. 食べる (taberu)

2. 書く (kaku)

3. 話す (hanasu)

4. する (suru)

5. 見る (miru)

Answers:

  1. 食べられる (taberareru)
    to be eaten
  2. 書かれる (kakareru)
    to be written
  3. 話される (hanasareru)
    to be spoken
  4. される (sareru)
    to be done
  5. 見られる (mirareru)
    to be seen

Exercise 2: Transform Active to Passive

Transform the following active sentences into passive sentences.

1. 先生が学生を褒めました。

Sensei ga gakusei o homemashita.

The teacher praised the student.

2. 犬が私を噛みました。

Inu ga watashi o kamimashita.

The dog bit me.

3. 彼女が手紙を書きました。

Kanojo ga tegami o kakimashita.

She wrote a letter.

4. 友達が私の秘密を話しました。

Tomodachi ga watashi no himitsu o hanashimashita.

My friend told my secret.

5. 誰かが私の自転車を盗みました。

Dareka ga watashi no jitensha o nusumimashita.

Someone stole my bicycle.

Answers:

  1. 学生は先生に褒められました。
    Gakusei wa sensei ni homeraremashita.
    The student was praised by the teacher.
  2. 私は犬に噛まれました。
    Watashi wa inu ni kamaremashita.
    I was bitten by the dog.
  3. 手紙は彼女に書かれました。
    Tegami wa kanojo ni kakaremashita.
    The letter was written by her.
  4. 私は友達に秘密を話されました。
    Watashi wa tomodachi ni himitsu o hanasaremashita.
    My secret was told by my friend. / I had my secret told by my friend.

    (This is a suffering passive)

  5. 私は自転車を盗まれました。
    Watashi wa jitensha o nusumaremashita.
    I had my bicycle stolen.

    (This is a suffering passive)

Exercise 3: Identify the Type of Passive

Identify whether each sentence is a direct passive, indirect passive, or suffering passive.

1. 私はその映画に感動されました。

Watashi wa sono eiga ni kandō saremashita.

I was moved by that movie.

2. 私は雨に降られました。

Watashi wa ame ni furaremashita.

I was rained on.

3. 私は友達にケーキを食べられました。

Watashi wa tomodachi ni kēki o taberaremashita.

My cake was eaten by my friend. / I had my cake eaten by my friend.

4. このレストランは多くの人に利用されています。

Kono resutoran wa ōku no hito ni riyō sarete imasu.

This restaurant is used by many people.

5. 私は子供に夜中に起こされました。

Watashi wa kodomo ni yonaka ni okosaremashita.

I was woken up by my child in the middle of the night.

Answers:

  1. Direct Passive - The movie directly moved me.
  2. Indirect Passive - I wasn't directly rained on, but was affected by the rain.
  3. Suffering Passive - My cake was eaten (and I was negatively affected).
  4. Direct Passive - The restaurant is directly used by people.
  5. Indirect/Suffering Passive - I was affected by the child waking me up (with negative nuance).
