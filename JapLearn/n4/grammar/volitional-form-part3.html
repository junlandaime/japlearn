Usage Patterns

The volitional form in Japanese has several important usage patterns. Let's explore the most common ways to use this grammar:

1. Personal Intention (〜よう)

When used in the first person, the plain volitional form often expresses the speaker's own intention or determination to do something.

Example:

明日から毎日運動しよう。

Ashita kara mainichi undou shiyou.

I'll exercise every day starting tomorrow.

Example:

もっと頑張ろう。

Motto ganbarou.

I'll try harder.

This usage is often monologic (talking to oneself) and expresses a decision the speaker has just made.

2. Invitation (〜ましょう)

The polite volitional form is commonly used to invite someone to do something together. This is equivalent to "let's" in English.

Example:

一緒に食べましょう。

Issho ni tabemashou.

Let's eat together.

Example:

明日の朝9時に会いましょう。

Ashita no asa 9-ji ni aimashou.

Let's meet tomorrow morning at 9 o'clock.

Note: The casual form 〜よう can also be used for invitations among friends, but 〜ましょう is more common and polite.

3. Suggestion (〜ましょうか)

Adding か (ka) to the volitional form turns it into a suggestion or offer to do something for someone.

Example:

手伝いましょうか。

Tetsudaimashou ka.

Shall I help you?

Example:

窓を開けましょうか。

Mado o akemashou ka.

Shall I open the window?

This pattern is often used in service situations or when offering assistance.

4. With と思う (to omou)

Combining the volitional form with と思う (to omou - "to think") emphasizes that the action is something you're considering or planning to do.

Example:

明日、図書館に行こうと思います。

Ashita, toshokan ni ikou to omoimasu.

I'm thinking of going to the library tomorrow.

Example:

今年の夏、日本へ行こうと思っています。

Kotoshi no natsu, Nihon e ikou to omotteimasu.

I'm planning to go to Japan this summer.

5. With としている (to shiteiru)

Combining the volitional form with としている (to shiteiru) indicates that you're making an effort or attempting to do something.

Example:

毎日、日本語を勉強しようとしています。

Mainichi, nihongo o benkyou shiyou to shiteimasu.

I'm trying to study Japanese every day.

Example:

健康的な生活を送ろうとしている。

Kenkouteki na seikatsu o okurou to shiteiru.

I'm trying to lead a healthy lifestyle.

Common Expressions with Volitional Form

There are several fixed expressions that use the volitional form:

～ようか

Used to suggest an action or ask for confirmation

行こうか。

Ikou ka.

Shall we go?

～ようではないか

A stronger suggestion or encouragement

一緒に頑張ろうではないか。

Issho ni ganbarou dewa nai ka.

Why don't we work hard together?

～ようと思う

Expressing intention

早く寝ようと思う。

Hayaku neyou to omou.

I think I'll go to bed early.

～ようとする

Trying to do something

ドアを開けようとした。

Doa o akeyou to shita.

I tried to open the door.
