Practice Exercises

Test your understanding of giving and receiving verbs with these practice exercises.

Exercise 1: Choose the Correct Verb

Select the appropriate verb (あげる, くれる, or もらう) for each sentence.

1. 私は友達に誕生日プレゼントを＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa tomodachi ni tanjoubi purezento o _______.

I gave a birthday present to my friend.

あげました (agemashita)

The speaker (I) is giving to someone else (friend), so あげる is used.

2. 先生は私に新しい本を＿＿＿＿＿。

Sensei wa watashi ni atarashii hon o _______.

The teacher gave me a new book.

くれました (kuremashita)

Someone else (teacher) is giving to the speaker (me), so くれる is used.

3. 私は母に花を＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa haha ni hana o _______.

I gave flowers to my mother.

あげました (agemashita)

The speaker (I) is giving to someone else (mother), so あげる is used, even though mother is in the speaker's in-group.

4. 私は友達にケーキを＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa tomodachi ni keeki o _______.

I received a cake from my friend.

もらいました (moraimashita)

The speaker (I) is receiving from someone else (friend), so もらう is used.

5. 母は弟におもちゃを＿＿＿＿＿。

Haha wa otouto ni omocha o _______.

My mother gave a toy to my younger brother.

あげました (agemashita)

Both mother and brother are in the speaker's in-group, but neither is the speaker, so あげる is used.

Exercise 2: Using ~て Form with Giving and Receiving Verbs

Complete each sentence with the appropriate ~て form expression.

1. 友達は私の宿題を手伝って＿＿＿＿＿。

Tomodachi wa watashi no shukudai o tetsudatte _______.

My friend helped me with my homework.

くれました (kuremashita)

Someone else (friend) did something for the speaker's benefit, so ~てくれる is used.

2. 私は子供に本を読んで＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa kodomo ni hon o yonde _______.

I read a book to the child.

あげました (agemashita)

The speaker did something for someone else's benefit, so ~てあげる is used.

3. 私は先生に日本語を教えて＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa sensei ni nihongo o oshiete _______.

I had the teacher teach me Japanese.

もらいました (moraimashita)

The speaker received the benefit of someone else's action, so ~てもらう is used.

Exercise 3: Using Honorific Forms

Replace the basic verbs with their appropriate honorific forms.

1. 私は社長にレポートをあげました。 → 私は社長にレポートを＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa shachou ni repooto o agemashita. → Watashi wa shachou ni repooto o _______.

さしあげました (sashiagemashita)

When giving to someone of higher status (company president), use the humble form さしあげる.

2. 先生は私にアドバイスをくれました。 → 先生は私にアドバイスを＿＿＿＿＿。

Sensei wa watashi ni adobaisu o kuremashita. → Sensei wa watashi ni adobaisu o _______.

くださいました (kudasaimashita)

When someone of higher status (teacher) gives something to you, use the honorific form くださる.

3. 私は先生に本をもらいました。 → 私は先生に本を＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa sensei ni hon o moraimashita. → Watashi wa sensei ni hon o _______.

いただきました (itadakimashita)

When receiving something from someone of higher status (teacher), use the humble form いただく.

Summary

Key Takeaways

  • • Japanese has three main giving and receiving verbs: あげる (to give from speaker to others), くれる (to give from others to speaker), and もらう (to receive by speaker from others).
  • • The choice of verb depends on the direction of the action and the relationship between the giver and receiver.
  • • These verbs have honorific forms (さしあげる, くださる, いただく) used to show respect or humility.
  • • The concept of "in-group" vs. "out-group" is crucial for using these verbs correctly.
  • • These verbs can be combined with the て-form to express doing something for someone's benefit or having someone do something for you.
