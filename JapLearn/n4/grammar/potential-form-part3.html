Usage Patterns

The potential form in Japanese has several important usage patterns that differ from English. Let's explore the most common ways to use this grammar:

1. Expressing Ability

The most common use of the potential form is to express one's ability to do something.

Example:

私は日本語が話せます。

Watashi wa nihongo ga hanasemasu.

I can speak Japanese.

Example:

彼は泳げません。

Kare wa oyogemasen.

He cannot swim.

Important Note: When using the potential form to express ability, the object particle often changes from を (o) to が (ga). This is a key difference from the regular verb form.

2. Expressing Possibility

The potential form can also express whether something is possible or not in a given situation.

Example:

この部屋で勉強できます。

Kono heya de benkyō dekimasu.

You can study in this room. (It's possible to study in this room.)

Example:

ここでタバコは吸えません。

Koko de tabako wa suemasen.

You cannot smoke here. (Smoking is not possible here.)

3. Asking for Permission

The potential form can be used to ask if something is allowed or possible, functioning as a polite way to ask for permission.

Example:

ここに座れますか。

Koko ni suwaremasuka.

Can I sit here? (Is it possible/allowed for me to sit here?)

Example:

写真を撮ってもいいですか。

Shashin o totte mo ii desu ka.

Can I take a picture? (Is it okay if I take a picture?)

Note: This example uses a different grammar pattern (〜てもいいですか) which is another common way to ask for permission.

4. Using できる with Nouns

To express ability with nouns, you can use the pattern: [Noun] + が + できる

Example:

私はピアノができます。

Watashi wa piano ga dekimasu.

I can play the piano.

Example:

彼は料理ができません。

Kare wa ryōri ga dekimasen.

He cannot cook.

5. Expressing Potential with Adjectives

Sometimes, the potential meaning is expressed using adjectives rather than the potential form of verbs.

Example:

この本は読みやすいです。

Kono hon wa yomiyasui desu.

This book is easy to read. (This book can be read easily.)

Example:

この漢字は書きにくいです。

Kono kanji wa kakinikui desu.

This kanji is difficult to write. (This kanji cannot be written easily.)

Particle Changes with Potential Form

One of the most important things to remember about the potential form is that it often changes the particle used with the object:

Regular Verb

私は日本語を話します。

Watashi wa nihongo o hanashimasu.

I speak Japanese.

Potential Form

私は日本語が話せます。

Watashi wa nihongo ga hanasemasu.

I can speak Japanese.

This particle change happens because in the potential form, the object (日本語/nihongo in this example) becomes the subject of the ability. However, in casual speech, many Japanese people still use を (o) with the potential form, so you might hear both patterns.
