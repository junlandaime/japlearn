Introduction to Prohibition Forms

In Japanese, there are several ways to express prohibition or to tell someone not to do something. The form you choose depends on the level of formality required, your relationship with the listener, and the social context.

This lesson covers the various prohibition forms in Japanese, from the most polite to the most casual, and explains when to use each one appropriately.

JLPT N4 Point: Understanding and being able to use different prohibition forms is essential for the JLPT N4 level. You should be familiar with both polite and casual forms, and know which contexts they are appropriate for.

Let's start by looking at the different ways to express prohibition in Japanese, from the most formal to the most casual:

Prohibition Form Formality Level Example
〜ないでください Polite ここで食べないでください。
Please don't eat here.
〜てはいけません Formal ここでタバコを吸ってはいけません。
Smoking is not allowed here.
〜ないほうがいいです Polite advisory その水は飲まないほうがいいです。
It's better not to drink that water.
〜ないで Casual そんなに心配しないで。
Don't worry so much.
〜てはだめ / 〜ちゃだめ Casual ここで遊んじゃだめ。
You shouldn't play here.
〜な Very casual / Rough 動くな！
Don't move!
