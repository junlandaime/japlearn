Exercise 3: Sentence Transformation

Transform each sentence by changing the transitive verb to its intransitive counterpart (or vice versa).

1. 私はドアを開けました。

Watashi wa doa o akemashita.

I opened the door.

ドアが開きました。

Doa ga akimashita.

The door opened.

2. 電気が消えました。

Denki ga kiemashita.

The light went out.

誰かが電気を消しました。

Dareka ga denki o keshimashita.

Someone turned off the light.

3. 先生は授業を始めました。

Sensei wa jugyō o hajimemashita.

The teacher started the class.

授業が始まりました。

Jugyō ga hajimarimashita.

The class started.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Using the wrong particle

❌ 私はドアが開けました。

Watashi wa doa ga akemashita.

✓ 私はドアを開けました。

Watashi wa doa o akemashita.

When using a transitive verb like 開ける (akeru), the object must be marked with を (o), not が (ga).

Mistake 2: Using the wrong verb in the pair

❌ ドアを開きました。

Doa o akimashita.

✓ ドアが開きました。

Doa ga akimashita.

開く (aku) is an intransitive verb, so it cannot take a direct object with を (o). Use 開ける (akeru) instead if you want to express that someone opened the door.

Mistake 3: Confusing similar verb pairs

❌ 私は電気を消えました。

Watashi wa denki o kiemashita.

✓ 私は電気を消しました。

Watashi wa denki o keshimashita.

消える (kieru) is intransitive and means "to go out/disappear." The transitive counterpart is 消す (kesu), which means "to turn off/extinguish."

Summary

Key Takeaways

  • • Transitive verbs (他動詞) describe actions done to something and take a direct object marked by を (o).
  • • Intransitive verbs (自動詞) describe actions that happen by themselves and typically use が (ga) to mark the subject.
  • • Many Japanese verbs come in transitive-intransitive pairs with related meanings.
  • • While there are some patterns to identify which is which, it's often best to memorize these pairs together.
  • • The choice between transitive and intransitive verbs can reflect the speaker's perspective on agency and responsibility.
