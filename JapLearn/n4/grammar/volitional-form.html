Volitional Form (～しよう)

Expressing intention, invitation, and suggestion in Japanese

Understanding the Volitional Form

N4 Level

The volitional form (意向形・いこうけい) in Japanese is used to express intention, invitation, or suggestion. It's similar to "let's" or "I will" in English, depending on the context. This versatile grammar point is essential for making plans, suggesting activities, and expressing your own intentions.

Key Point: The volitional form can have different meanings depending on the context and intonation. It can express your own will ("I will"), an invitation ("Let's"), or a suggestion ("Why don't we").

The volitional form is commonly used in everyday Japanese conversation, especially when making plans with friends, suggesting activities, or expressing your own intentions. Let's look at some examples:

Intention

Expressing Your Will

Used to express what you intend or have decided to do.

Example:

明日、早く起きよう。

Ashita, hayaku okiyou.

I'll wake up early tomorrow.

Invitation

Inviting Others

Used to invite someone to do something together.

Example:

映画を見に行きましょう。

Eiga o mi ni ikimashou.

Let's go see a movie.

Suggestion

Making Suggestions

Used to suggest a course of action.

Example:

この問題を一緒に解決しよう。

Kono mondai o issho ni kaiketsushiyou.

Let's solve this problem together.
