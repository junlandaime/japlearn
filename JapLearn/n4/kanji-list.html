N4 Kanji Overview

The JLPT N4 level requires knowledge of approximately 300 kanji characters. This is in addition to the 100 kanji from the N5 level, bringing your total to around 400 kanji characters. These kanji appear frequently in everyday reading materials and are essential for intermediate Japanese proficiency.

Study Tip: Focus on learning kanji in context through vocabulary words rather than memorizing them in isolation. This approach will help you recognize and use kanji more naturally in your reading and writing.

Kanji Study Resources

To help you master the N4 level kanji, we've compiled some useful resources and study techniques:

Effective Study Methods

  • Spaced Repetition: Review kanji at increasing intervals to improve long-term retention
  • Vocabulary Context: Learn kanji in the context of common vocabulary words
  • Radical Analysis: Break down complex kanji into their component parts
  • Writing Practice: Write kanji repeatedly to build muscle memory
  • Mnemonic Devices: Create stories or visual associations for difficult kanji

Recommended Tools

  • Anki: Create digital flashcards with spaced repetition
  • Kanji Dictionary: "A Guide to Remembering Japanese Characters" by Kenneth G. Henshall
  • Kanji Practice Books: "Basic Kanji Book Vol. 2" for structured writing practice
  • Jisho.org: Online Japanese dictionary with kanji lookup by radical
  • Language Exchange: Practice using kanji in real conversations
