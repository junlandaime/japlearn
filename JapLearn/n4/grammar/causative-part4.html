Summary

In this lesson, we've learned about the causative form in Japanese:

  • Formation:
    • Group 1 (Godan) verbs: う → あ + せる
    • Group 2 (Ichidan) verbs: る → させる
    • Irregular verbs: する → させる, 来る → 来させる
  • Meanings:
    • To make someone do something (compulsion, command)
    • To let someone do something (permission, allowing)
  • Sentence Structure:
    • With transitive verbs: Person A は Person B に Object を Verb (causative)
    • With intransitive verbs: Person A は Person B を Verb (causative)
  • Combinations:
    • Causative + てあげる/てくれる (doing a favor)
    • Causative + てもらう (getting someone to do something)
    • Causative + negative (not letting/making someone do something)
    • Causative + potential (being able to let/make someone do something)

The causative form is an important grammatical structure in Japanese that allows you to express relationships of authority, permission, and control. Understanding the nuances between "making" and "letting" someone do something is crucial for using this form correctly in various contexts.
