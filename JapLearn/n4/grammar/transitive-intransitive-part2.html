Common Transitive-Intransitive Verb Pairs

Many Japanese verbs come in transitive-intransitive pairs. While there are some patterns to help you identify which is which, it's often best to memorize these pairs together. Here are some of the most common pairs you'll encounter at the N4 level:

Open/Close Pairs

Transitive

開ける (あける)

akeru - to open (something)

Intransitive

開く (あく)

aku - to open (by itself)

Transitive

閉める (しめる)

shimeru - to close (something)

Intransitive

閉まる (しまる)

shimaru - to close (by itself)

Start/Stop Pairs

Transitive

始める (はじめる)

hajimeru - to start (something)

Intransitive

始まる (はじまる)

hajimaru - to begin (by itself)

Transitive

終える (おえる)

oeru - to finish (something)

Intransitive

終わる (おわる)

owaru - to end (by itself)

Turn On/Off Pairs

Transitive

つける

tsukeru - to turn on (something)

Intransitive

つく

tsuku - to turn on (by itself)

Transitive

消す (けす)

kesu - to turn off/extinguish

Intransitive

消える (きえる)

kieru - to go out/disappear

Break/Fall Pairs

Transitive

壊す (こわす)

kowasu - to break (something)

Intransitive

壊れる (こわれる)

kowareru - to break (by itself)

Transitive

落とす (おとす)

otosu - to drop (something)

Intransitive

落ちる (おちる)

ochiru - to fall

Pattern Recognition

While there are many exceptions, you can often identify transitive-intransitive pairs by looking at the verb endings. Here are some common patterns:

Transitive → Intransitive

  • ~eru → ~aru: 開ける → 開く (akeru → aku)
  • ~u → ~eru: 壊す → 壊れる (kowasu → kowareru)
  • ~eru → ~u: 見せる → 見る (miseru → miru)

Common Suffixes

  • ~す (~su): Often transitive
  • ~る (~ru): Often intransitive
  • ~aru: Almost always intransitive

Sentence Patterns

Understanding how to use transitive and intransitive verbs in sentences is crucial. Here are the basic patterns:

Transitive Verb Pattern

[Person] は [Object] を [Transitive Verb]

Example:

私は窓を開けます。

Watashi wa mado o akemasu.

I open the window.

Example:

先生は授業を始めました。

Sensei wa jugyō o hajimemashita.

The teacher started the class.

Intransitive Verb Pattern

[Thing] が [Intransitive Verb]

Example:

窓が開きます。

Mado ga akimasu.

The window opens.

Example:

授業が始まりました。

Jugyō ga hajimarimashita.

The class started.

Important Note: In Japanese, the choice between transitive and intransitive verbs often reflects the speaker's perspective on agency and responsibility. Using an intransitive verb can sometimes be a way to avoid assigning blame or responsibility for an action.
