Practice Exercises

Test your understanding of the volitional form with these practice exercises.

Exercise 1: Conjugate to the Volitional Form

Convert each verb to its plain volitional form.

1. 行く (いく) - to go

行こう (いこう)

Ikou - let's go

2. 食べる (たべる) - to eat

食べよう (たべよう)

Tabeyou - let's eat

3. 待つ (まつ) - to wait

待とう (まとう)

Matou - let's wait

4. する - to do

しよう

Shiyou - let's do

5. 来る (くる) - to come

来よう (こよう)

Koyou - let's come

Exercise 2: Convert to Polite Volitional

Change each plain volitional form to its polite form.

1. 行こう (いこう)

行きましょう (いきましょう)

Ikimashou

2. 食べよう (たべよう)

食べましょう (たべましょう)

Tabemashou

3. しよう

しましょう

Shimashou

Exercise 3: Identify the Usage

Determine whether each sentence expresses intention, invitation, or suggestion.

1. 明日、早く起きよう。

Ashita, hayaku okiyou.

Intention

This is expressing the speaker's own intention to wake up early tomorrow.

2. 一緒に映画を見に行きましょう。

Issho ni eiga o mi ni ikimashou.

Invitation

This is inviting someone to go see a movie together.

3. 手伝いましょうか。

Tetsudaimashou ka.

Suggestion/Offer

This is offering to help someone.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Confusing Group 1 and Group 2 conjugations

❌ 話ろう (はなろう)

Hanarou

✓ 話そう (はなそう)

Hanasou

For Group 1 verbs, you must change the final う-sound to the corresponding お-sound and add う. Don't just add ろう to the stem.

Mistake 2: Using the wrong context

❌ 先生は明日来ようと言いました。

Sensei wa ashita koyou to iimashita.

✓ 先生は明日来ると言いました。

Sensei wa ashita kuru to iimashita.

The volitional form is used for the speaker's own intentions or for invitations. When reporting what someone else said they will do, use the plain form instead.

Mistake 3: Creating a direct negative volitional

❌ 行かないよう

Ikanai you

✓ 行くのはやめよう

Iku no wa yameyou

Remember that the volitional form doesn't have a direct negative form. Use the structure "verb + のはやめよう" instead.

Summary

Key Takeaways

  • • The volitional form expresses intention ("I will"), invitation ("let's"), or suggestion ("shall we").
  • • Group 1 (U) verbs: Change the final う-sound to the corresponding お-sound + う (話す → 話そう).
  • • Group 2 (RU) verbs: Remove る and add よう (食べる → 食べよう).
  • • Irregular verbs: する → しよう, 来る → 来よう.
  • • The polite form is created by adding ましょう to the verb stem.
  • • To express "let's not do something," use the structure "verb + のはやめよう".
