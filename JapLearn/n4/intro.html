JLPT N4 Introduction

Welcome to the next step in your Japanese language journey!

What is JLPT N4?

The Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) N4 is the second level from the bottom in the five-level JLPT system. It's designed for learners who have progressed beyond the basic N5 level and are ready to tackle more intermediate concepts in Japanese.

At the N4 level, you'll build upon the foundation you established at N5, learning more complex grammar patterns, expanding your vocabulary to around 1,500-2,000 words, and mastering approximately 300 kanji characters.

According to the JLPT website: N4 certifies that you "can understand basic Japanese" and are able to read and understand passages on familiar daily topics written in basic vocabulary and kanji. You should also be able to listen and comprehend conversations encountered in daily life and generally follow their contents.

Key Differences from N5

  • More complex grammar: Learn conditional forms, causative, passive, and more nuanced expressions
  • Expanded vocabulary: Nearly double the vocabulary compared to N5
  • More kanji: About 300 kanji total (compared to 100 at N5 level)
  • Longer readings: Texts with more complex structures and content
  • Natural dialogue: Listening exercises with less simplified language

What You'll Be Able to Do

  • Hold basic conversations about daily life topics
  • Express a wider range of emotions and opinions
  • Read simple articles, advertisements, and short texts
  • Write basic emails or messages with more complex expressions
  • Understand announcements in public spaces like stations
  • Navigate various daily situations in Japan with less difficulty

N4 Sample Grammar Point

Here's a taste of the type of grammar you'll learn in the N4 course:

Potential Form: ~ことができます

N4 Level

The potential form is used to express the ability to do something. In N5, you learned the simple potential form of verbs (e.g., 食べられる - can eat). In N4, we'll explore the pattern 〜ことができます, which offers another way to express ability.

Example:

日本語で話すことができます。

Nihongo de hanasu koto ga dekimasu.

I can speak in Japanese.

Example:

漢字を読むことができますか。

Kanji o yomu koto ga dekimasu ka.

Can you read kanji?

Prerequisites for N4

Before diving into the N4 level, we recommend you have the following foundation:

  • Hiragana & Katakana: Confident reading and writing of both Japanese syllabaries
  • Basic Kanji: Familiarity with approximately 100 basic kanji (N5 level)
  • N5 Grammar: Understanding of basic sentence structures, particles, and tenses
  • Basic Vocabulary: Knowledge of around 800-1000 common Japanese words
  • Simple Conversations: Ability to engage in basic everyday conversations
