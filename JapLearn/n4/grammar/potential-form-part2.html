How to Form the Potential

The conjugation rules for the potential form depend on which verb group the verb belongs to. In Japanese, verbs are categorized into three groups:

Group 1: U-Verbs (五段動詞・ごだんどうし)

These are verbs whose dictionary form ends in a う-sound (u, ku, gu, su, tsu, nu, bu, mu, ru). To form the potential:

Change the final う-sound to the corresponding え-sound + る

Dictionary Form Potential Form Meaning
話す (はなす) 話せる (はなせる) to speak → can speak
書く (かく) 書ける (かける) to write → can write
泳ぐ (およぐ) 泳げる (およげる) to swim → can swim
待つ (まつ) 待てる (まてる) to wait → can wait
読む (よむ) 読める (よめる) to read → can read
売る (うる) 売れる (うれる) to sell → can sell

Group 2: RU-Verbs (一段動詞・いちだんどうし)

These are verbs whose dictionary form ends in いる (iru) or える (eru). To form the potential:

Remove the final る and add られる

Dictionary Form Potential Form Meaning
食べる (たべる) 食べられる (たべられる) to eat → can eat
見る (みる) 見られる (みられる) to see → can see
起きる (おきる) 起きられる (おきられる) to wake up → can wake up
出る (でる) 出られる (でられる) to exit → can exit

Note: In colloquial Japanese, Group 2 verbs often use a shortened form where られる becomes れる. For example, 食べられる becomes 食べれる. This is very common in spoken Japanese but is considered incorrect in formal writing.

Group 3: Irregular Verbs (不規則動詞・ふきそくどうし)

There are only two irregular verbs in Japanese, and they have special potential forms:

Dictionary Form Potential Form Meaning
する (する) できる (できる) to do → can do
来る (くる) 来られる (こられる) to come → can come

Note that する (suru) has a completely different word for its potential form: できる (dekiru). This is one of the most common verbs in Japanese.

Conjugating the Potential Form

Once you've formed the potential form of a verb, it conjugates like a regular Group 2 verb:

Affirmative Forms

  • Plain Present: 話せる (hanaseru) - can speak
  • Polite Present: 話せます (hanasemasu) - can speak
  • Plain Past: 話せた (hanaseta) - could speak
  • Polite Past: 話せました (hanasemashita) - could speak

Negative Forms

  • Plain Present: 話せない (hanasenai) - cannot speak
  • Polite Present: 話せません (hanasemasen) - cannot speak
  • Plain Past: 話せなかった (hanasenakatta) - could not speak
  • Polite Past: 話せませんでした (hanasemasen deshita) - could not speak
