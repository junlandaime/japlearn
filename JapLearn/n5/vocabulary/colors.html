Introduction to Colors in Japanese

Colors (色・いろ・iro) are an essential part of daily vocabulary in any language. In Japanese, color words are used in a variety of contexts, from describing objects to expressing cultural concepts. Learning color vocabulary will help you describe the world around you and navigate everyday situations.

Japanese has two sets of color terms: native Japanese words and those borrowed from Chinese. For N5 level, you'll mostly focus on the basic colors that are commonly used in everyday conversations.

Cultural Note: Colors have significant cultural meanings in Japan. For example, red (赤・あか) is associated with energy and vitality, while white (白・しろ) represents purity and is often used in ceremonial contexts.

Example Sentences:

この本は赤いです。

Kono hon wa akai desu.

This book is red.

彼女は黒い服を着ています。

Kanojo wa kuroi fuku o kite imasu.

She is wearing black clothes.

Sora wa aoi desu.

The sky is blue.

Language Note: When using colors as adjectives, you need to add the suffix -i (い). For example, "red" (赤・あか) becomes "red" (as an adjective) 赤い (あかい・akai).

Color Adjectives

In Japanese, colors can function as nouns (名詞・めいし・meishi) or adjectives (形容詞・けいようし・keiyōshi). When used as adjectives, most colors add the suffix -i (い).

Color (Noun) Adjective Form Romaji Example Usage Audio
白 (しろ) 白い (しろい) Shiroi 白い雪 (shiroi yuki - white snow)
黒 (くろ) 黒い (くろい) Kuroi 黒い猫 (kuroi neko - black cat)
赤 (あか) 赤い (あかい) Akai 赤いりんご (akai ringo - red apple)
青 (あお) 青い (あおい) Aoi 青い空 (aoi sora - blue sky)
黄色 (きいろ) 黄色い (きいろい) Kiiroi 黄色いバナナ (kiiroi banana - yellow banana)
緑 (みどり) 緑の (みどりの) Midori no 緑の葉 (midori no ha - green leaf)
茶色 (ちゃいろ) 茶色の (ちゃいろの) Chairo no 茶色の机 (chairo no tsukue - brown desk)

Grammar Note: Notice that some colors (like midori and chairo) use the particle "no" (の) instead of the "i" (い) ending when used as adjectives. This is because they are technically nouns being used in an adjectival way, not true adjectives.

Color Shades and Variations

Japanese also has words to describe different shades and brightness of colors. Here are some common modifiers:

Japanese Romaji English Example Audio
薄 (うす) Usu- Light/Pale 薄緑 (usumidori - light green)
濃 (こ) Ko- Dark/Deep 濃青 (koao - deep blue)
明るい (あかるい) Akarui Bright 明るい赤 (akarui aka - bright red)
暗い (くらい) Kurai Dark 暗い青 (kurai ao - dark blue)
鮮やかな (あざやかな) Azayaka na Vivid/Bright 鮮やかな緑 (azayaka na midori - vivid green)
くすんだ Kusunda Dull/Faded くすんだ紫 (kusunda murasaki - dull purple)

Example Sentences:

この服は薄青です。

Kono fuku wa usuao desu.

This clothing is light blue.

彼女は鮮やかな赤いドレスを着ています。

Kanojo wa azayaka na akai doresu o kite imasu.

She is wearing a vivid red dress.
