N5 Reading Practice

Improve your Japanese reading comprehension with these JLPT N5 level exercises.

Introduction to Reading Practice

Reading comprehension is a crucial part of the JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test). At the N5 level, you'll need to be able to understand short texts, signs, and simple messages written in hiragana, katakana, and basic kanji.

This page offers a variety of reading exercises that mimic the format of the JLPT N5 test. Each exercise includes a short passage or text followed by questions to test your comprehension. The exercises gradually increase in difficulty to help you build your skills.

Reading Tip: When reading Japanese text, try to understand the main idea first before focusing on individual words. Look for familiar grammar patterns, and use context clues to help you understand unfamiliar vocabulary. You can hover over underlined words for meaning hints.

How to Use This Page:

  1. Read the passage or text carefully
  2. Answer the questions based on what you read
  3. Check your answers by clicking the "Check Answers" button
  4. Review the explanations for any questions you got wrong
  5. Move on to the next exercise when you're ready

Exercise 1: Short Message

In this exercise, you'll read a simple message and answer questions about its content. Take your time to understand the text and then select the best answer for each question.

メッセージ (Message):

田中Tanaka (family name)さんへ

こんにちは。元気genki - fine/healthyですか。
明日ashita - tomorrowの会議kaigi - meetingは9時ji - o'clockから11時までです。
新しいatarashii - new資料shiryō - documents/materialsを持ってmotte - bring/carryきてください。
教室kyōshitsu - classroomは302です。

よろしくお願negai - requestいします。
山田Yamada (family name)

Translation:
To Tanaka-san,

Hello. How are you?
Tomorrow's meeting is from 9 o'clock to 11 o'clock.
Please bring the new documents.
The classroom is 302.

Thank you/Best regards.
Yamada

Question 1

What time does the meeting start?

Correct Answer: A. 9:00

Explanation: The message states "明日の会議は9時から11時までです" which means "Tomorrow's meeting is from 9 o'clock to 11 o'clock." So the meeting starts at 9:00.

Question 2

What should Tanaka-san bring to the meeting?

Correct Answer: C. New documents

Explanation: The message states "新しい資料を持ってきてください" which means "Please bring the new documents."

Question 3

Where will the meeting take place?

Correct Answer: B. Room 302

Explanation: The message states "教室は302です" which means "The classroom is 302."
