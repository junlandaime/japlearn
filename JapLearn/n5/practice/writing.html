N5 Writing Practice

Learn to write hiragana, katakana, and basic kanji characters for JLPT N5 level.

Introduction to Writing Practice

Writing is a fundamental skill for learning Japanese. At the N5 level, you'll need to be able to write hiragana, katakana, and about 100 basic kanji characters. This page offers a variety of writing exercises to help you practice and improve your Japanese writing skills.

The exercises are designed to help you learn the correct stroke order, practice writing characters, and eventually form complete sentences. You can practice on paper and then check your writing against the examples provided.

Writing Tip: Always follow the correct stroke order when writing Japanese characters. Stroke order is important for producing well-formed characters and developing proper writing habits. It also helps with recognition and reading speed later on.

How to Use This Page:

  1. Study the character and its stroke order
  2. Practice writing the character on paper, following the correct stroke order
  3. Check your writing against the example
  4. Practice writing words and sentences using the characters you've learned
  5. Review regularly to maintain your skills
