Usage and Examples

The causative form has various nuances and uses in Japanese. Let's explore the different contexts in which it is used:

Making Someone Do Something

When the causative form is used to express that someone is making another person do something, it can range from a direct command to a gentle encouragement.

先生は生徒に漢字を書かせました。

Sensei wa seito ni kanji o kakasemashita.

The teacher made the students write kanji.

上司は部下に残業させました。

Jōshi wa buka ni zangyō sasemashita.

The boss made the subordinates work overtime.

親は子供に野菜を食べさせました。

Oya wa kodomo ni yasai o tabesasemashita.

The parents made the child eat vegetables.

コーチは選手に毎日走らせます。

Kōchi wa senshu ni mainichi hashirasemasu.

The coach makes the athletes run every day.

Cultural Note: In Japanese culture, the causative form when used to express "making someone do something" can sometimes have a negative connotation, especially in workplace or hierarchical relationships. It can imply that the person in authority is imposing their will on someone of lower status.

Letting Someone Do Something

When the causative form is used to express that someone is letting another person do something, it indicates permission or allowing an action to happen.

親は子供を友達の家に泊まらせました。

Oya wa kodomo o tomodachi no ie ni tomarasemashita.

The parents let the child stay at a friend's house.

先生は学生を早く帰らせました。

Sensei wa gakusei o hayaku kaerasemashita.

The teacher let the students go home early.

医者は患者を少し休ませました。

Isha wa kanja o sukoshi yasumasemashita.

The doctor let the patient rest a little.

母は子供にケーキを食べさせてくれました。

Haha wa kodomo ni kēki o tabesasete kuremashita.

The mother let the child eat cake (as a favor).

Causative with Other Forms

The causative form can be combined with other grammatical forms to express more complex meanings:

1. Causative + て form + auxiliary verbs

子供を遊ばせておきました。

Kodomo o asobasete okimashita.

I let the child play (and left it at that).

学生に本を読ませてみました。

Gakusei ni hon o yomasete mimashita.

I tried making the students read the book.

2. Causative + negative form

子供にテレビを見させませんでした。

Kodomo ni terebi o misasemasen deshita.

I didn't let the child watch TV.

先生は生徒を教室から出させません。

Sensei wa seito o kyōshitsu kara desasemasen.

The teacher doesn't let the students leave the classroom.

3. Causative + potential form

子供を一人で行かせられません。

Kodomo o hitori de ikaseraremasen.

I cannot let the child go alone.

彼に好きなことをさせられるでしょう。

Kare ni suki na koto o saserareru deshō.

I think I can let him do what he wants.

Tip: When combining the causative with other forms, the causative form comes first, followed by the other grammatical forms. For example, causative + potential = させられる (saserareru).
