Practical Usage of Keigo

Understanding when to use each type of keigo is crucial for proper communication in Japanese. Let's look at some common situations and how keigo is used in them:

When to Use Each Type of Keigo

Situation Type of Keigo Example
Talking to a stranger or someone you don't know well Teineigo こんにちは。お元気ですか？
Konnichiwa. O-genki desu ka?
Hello. How are you?
Talking about your boss to a colleague Sonkeigo 部長は今、会議にいらっしゃいます。
Buchō wa ima, kaigi ni irasshaimasu.
The department head is in a meeting now.
Talking about yourself to your boss Kenjougo 明日、報告書を提出いたします。
Ashita, hōkokusho o teishutsu itashimasu.
I will submit the report tomorrow.
Customer service interactions Sonkeigo (to customer) + Kenjougo (about yourself) 少々お待ちになってください。すぐ確認いたします。
Shōshō o-machi ni natte kudasai. Sugu kakunin itashimasu.
Please wait a moment. I will check right away.
Talking about a third party to your boss Teineigo (for neutral third parties) 田中さんは明日、来ます。
Tanaka-san wa ashita, kimasu.
Mr. Tanaka will come tomorrow.

Key Point: The choice of keigo depends on the relationship between:

  1. The speaker (you)
  2. The listener (who you're talking to)
  3. The subject (who you're talking about)

Talking About Someone of Higher Status

  • Use sonkeigo for their actions
  • Use respectful titles (先生, 部長, etc.)
  • Avoid casual language

Talking About Yourself

  • Use kenjougo when speaking to someone of higher status
  • Use teineigo when speaking to strangers or acquaintances
  • Never use sonkeigo for yourself

Combining Different Types of Keigo

In real conversations, you'll often need to use different types of keigo in the same sentence or dialogue:

Example: Speaking to your boss about a meeting

部長、明日の会議に何時にいらっしゃいますか？私は9時に会議室に参ります。

Buchō, ashita no kaigi ni nanji ni irasshaimasu ka? Watashi wa ku-ji ni kaigishitsu ni mairimasu.

Manager, what time will you come to tomorrow's meeting? I will come to the meeting room at 9 o'clock.

  • いらっしゃいます - Sonkeigo for "come" (about the boss)
  • 参ります - Kenjougo for "go" (about yourself)

Example: Customer service interaction

お客様、こちらの商品はいかがでしょうか。ご質問がございましたら、お答えいたします。

Okyaku-sama, kochira no shōhin wa ikaga deshō ka. Go-shitsumon ga gozaimashitara, o-kotae itashimasu.

Customer, how about this product? If you have any questions, I will answer them.

  • お客様 - Respectful term for "customer"
  • いかがでしょうか - Polite expression for "how about"
  • ご質問がございましたら - Humble form of "if there are questions"
  • お答えいたします - Humble form of "will answer"

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Identify the Type of Keigo

Identify whether each sentence uses teineigo, sonkeigo, or kenjougo.

1. 先生はもう食事を召し上がりましたか。

Sensei wa mō shokuji o meshiagarimashita ka.

Has the teacher already eaten?

2. 私は明日、東京に参ります。

Watashi wa ashita, Tōkyō ni mairimasu.

I will go to Tokyo tomorrow.

3. この本は面白いです。

Kono hon wa omoshiroi desu.

This book is interesting.

4. 社長はいつお戻りになりますか。

Shachō wa itsu o-modori ni narimasu ka.

When will the company president return?

5. ご質問がございましたら、お知らせください。

Go-shitsumon ga gozaimashitara, o-shirase kudasai.

If you have any questions, please let me know.

Answers:

  1. Sonkeigo - 召し上がる is the respectful form of 食べる (to eat)
  2. Kenjougo - 参る is the humble form of 行く (to go)
  3. Teineigo - Uses the polite form です
  4. Sonkeigo - お戻りになる is the respectful pattern for 戻る (to return)
  5. Kenjougo - ございます is the humble form of あります (there is)

Exercise 2: Transform to Appropriate Keigo

Transform the following plain sentences into the appropriate type of keigo based on the context.

1. Transform to sonkeigo (talking about your teacher):

先生は本を読む。

Sensei wa hon o yomu.

The teacher reads a book.

2. Transform to kenjougo (talking about yourself to your boss):

私は報告書を書く。

Watashi wa hōkokusho o kaku.

I write a report.

3. Transform to teineigo (talking to a stranger):

これは新しい本だ。

Kore wa atarashii hon da.

This is a new book.

4. Transform to sonkeigo (asking your boss):

部長はいつ来るか。

Buchō wa itsu kuru ka.

When will the manager come?

5. Transform to kenjougo (offering something to a customer):

お茶を持ってくる。

Ocha o motte kuru.

I will bring tea.

Answers:

  1. 先生は本をお読みになります。
    Sensei wa hon o o-yomi ni narimasu.
    The teacher reads a book. (respectful)
  2. 私は報告書を書かせていただきます。
    Watashi wa hōkokusho o kakasete itadakimasu.
    I write a report. (humble)

    Alternative: 私は報告書を作成いたします。 (Watashi wa hōkokusho o sakusei itashimasu.)

  3. これは新しい本です。
    Kore wa atarashii hon desu.
    This is a new book. (polite)
  4. 部長はいついらっしゃいますか。
    Buchō wa itsu irasshaimasu ka.
    When will the manager come? (respectful)
  5. お茶をお持ちいたします。
    Ocha o o-mochi itashimasu.
    I will bring tea. (humble)

Exercise 3: Complete the Dialogue

Complete the following dialogue between an employee and a manager using appropriate keigo.

Dialogue:

社員：部長、おはようございます。今日の会議は何時から＿＿＿＿＿＿＿＿？

Shain: Buchō, ohayō gozaimasu. Kyō no kaigi wa nanji kara _____________?

Employee: Good morning, Manager. What time does today's meeting _____________?

部長：9時からだよ。君は資料を＿＿＿＿＿＿＿＿？

Buchō: Ku-ji kara da yo. Kimi wa shiryō o _____________?

Manager: It's from 9 o'clock. Have you _______________ the documents?

社員：はい、昨日、資料を＿＿＿＿＿＿＿＿。会議室には8時45分に＿＿＿＿＿＿＿＿。

Shain: Hai, kinō, shiryō o _______________. Kaigishitsu ni wa hachi-ji yonjūgo-fun ni _______________.

Employee: Yes, I _______________ the documents yesterday. I will _______________ the meeting room at 8:45.

Answers:

社員：部長、おはようございます。今日の会議は何時から始まりますか？

Shain: Buchō, ohayō gozaimasu. Kyō no kaigi wa nanji kara hajimarimasu ka?

Employee: Good morning, Manager. What time does today's meeting start?

部長：9時からだよ。君は資料を準備した？

Buchō: Ku-ji kara da yo. Kimi wa shiryō o junbi shita?

Manager: It's from 9 o'clock. Have you prepared the documents?

社員：はい、昨日、資料をご用意いたしました。会議室には8時45分に参ります。

Shain: Hai, kinō, shiryō o go-yōi itashimashita. Kaigishitsu ni wa hachi-ji yonjūgo-fun ni mairimasu.

Employee: Yes, I prepared the documents yesterday. I will go to the meeting room at 8:45.

Explanation: The employee uses kenjougo (ご用意いたしました, 参ります) when talking about their own actions to show respect to the manager. The manager uses casual language because they are of higher status.
