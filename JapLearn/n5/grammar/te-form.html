Te-Form

The versatile て form in Japanese grammar

Introduction to the Te-Form

The Te-form (て形, te-kei) is one of the most important and versatile grammar forms in Japanese. It serves as a connector and foundation for many grammar structures and has multiple uses in everyday conversation.

The te-form gets its name from the fact that verbs in this form end with either て (te) or で (de). Understanding how to form and use the te-form is crucial for advancing your Japanese language skills.

Note: The te-form is not a tense by itself. Rather, it's a conjugation form that connects to other grammar structures or verbs to create different meanings.

How to Form the Te-Form

The te-form is created from the dictionary form of verbs through specific conjugation patterns. The process differs slightly depending on the verb group.

Group 1 (Godan) Verbs

For Group 1 (Godan) verbs, the conjugation rule depends on the final kana in the dictionary form:

Group 1 (Godan) Te-Form Rules

To remember the patterns:

  • う、つ、る → って: The kana becomes small っ + て
  • む、ぶ、ぬ → んで: The kana is replaced with ん + で
  • く → いて: The kana is replaced with い + て
  • ぐ → いで: The kana is replaced with い + で
  • す → して: The kana is replaced with し + て

Note: 行く (iku - to go) is an exception and becomes 行って (itte)

Group 2 (Ichidan) Verbs

For Group 2 (Ichidan) verbs, the conjugation is simple:

Group 2 (Ichidan) Te-Form Rule

Remove the final る (ru) and add て (te)

Examples:

  • 食べる (taberu - to eat) → 食べて (tabete)
  • 見る (miru - to see) → 見て (mite)
  • 起きる (okiru - to wake up) → 起きて (okite)

Group 3 (Irregular) Verbs

For the two irregular verbs:

Group 3 (Irregular) Te-Form Rules

These need to be memorized:

  • する (suru - to do) → して (shite)
  • 来る (kuru - to come) → 来て (kite)

For compound verbs using する:

  • 勉強する (benkyō suru - to study) → 勉強して (benkyō shite)
  • 運転する (unten suru - to drive) → 運転して (unten shite)

Adjectives and Nouns in Te-Form

Adjectives and nouns can also be conjugated into a te-form equivalent:

Type Rule Example Te-Form Equivalent
い-Adjectives Replace い with くて 高い (takai - expensive) 高くて (takakute)
な-Adjectives Add で 静か (shizuka - quiet) 静かで (shizuka de)
Nouns Add で 学生 (gakusei - student) 学生で (gakusei de)

Usage of the Te-Form

The te-form has many important functions in Japanese. Here are the main uses:

Connecting Actions (And, Then)

The te-form connects two or more actions that happen in sequence or simultaneously.

Example:

朝ご飯を食べて、学校に行きます。

Asa gohan o tabete, gakkō ni ikimasu.

I eat breakfast and go to school.

Making Requests

The te-form by itself can be used to make polite requests.

Example:

ここに名前を書いて。

Koko ni namae o kaite.

Please write your name here.

Continuous Actions (〜ています)

Te-form + いる/います (iru/imasu) expresses ongoing actions or states.

Example:

彼は今テレビを見ています。

Kare wa ima terebi o mite imasu.

He is watching TV now.

Permission or Prohibition

Te-form + も (mo) or はいけない (wa ikenai) expresses permission or prohibition.

Examples:

ここで写真を撮ってもいいです。

Koko de shashin o totte mo ii desu.

You may take pictures here.

教室で食べてはいけません。

Kyōshitsu de tabete wa ikemasen.

You must not eat in the classroom.

Giving and Receiving Actions

Te-form + あげる/くれる/もらう (ageru/kureru/morau) expresses giving or receiving actions.

Examples:

友達に本を貸してあげました。

Tomodachi ni hon o kashite agemashita.

I lent a book to my friend.

母が弁当を作ってくれました。

Haha ga bentō o tsukutte kuremashita.

My mother made a bento for me.

Preparation or Advance Action

Te-form + おく (oku) expresses doing something in advance or as preparation.

Example:

明日の会議の資料を準備しておきます。

Ashita no kaigi no shiryō o junbi shite okimasu.

I will prepare the materials for tomorrow's meeting in advance.

Completed or Regrettable Actions

Te-form + しまう (shimau) expresses a completed action or regrettable outcome.

Example:

宿題を忘れてしまいました。

Shukudai o wasurete shimaimashita.

I forgot my homework (regrettably).

Trying Something

Te-form + みる (miru) expresses trying to do something or seeing how it goes.

Example:

この料理を作ってみました。

Kono ryōri o tsukutte mimashita.

I tried making this dish.

Tip: The te-form is often called the "glue" of Japanese grammar because it connects so many different grammar points. Mastering this form will significantly improve your conversational ability.

Common Expressions with Te-Form

Here are some common expressions and phrases that use the te-form:

Expression Meaning Example
〜てください
(~te kudasai)		 Please do ~ 窓を開けてください。
Mado o akete kudasai.
Please open the window.
〜ていただけませんか
(~te itadakemasen ka)		 Could you please do ~ (very polite) 手伝っていただけませんか。
Tetsudatte itadakemasen ka.
Could you please help me?
〜てから
(~te kara)		 After doing ~ 宿題をしてから、テレビを見ます。
Shukudai o shite kara, terebi o mimasu.
I'll watch TV after doing my homework.
〜ても
(~te mo)		 Even if ~ 雨が降っても、行きます。
Ame ga futte mo, ikimasu.
Even if it rains, I will go.
〜てもいいですか
(~te mo ii desu ka)		 May I ~? ここに座ってもいいですか。
Koko ni suwatte mo ii desu ka.
May I sit here?
〜ている間
(~te iru aida)		 While ~ 寝ている間に、泥棒が入りました。
Nete iru aida ni, dorobō ga hairimashita.
While I was sleeping, a thief broke in.

Practice Exercises

Test your understanding of the te-form with these exercises:

Exercise 1: Convert to Te-Form

Convert the following verbs to their te-form:

  1. 読む (yomu - to read) 読んで (yonde)
  2. 話す (hanasu - to speak) 話して (hanashite)
  3. 帰る (kaeru - to return) 帰って (kaette)
  4. 食べる (taberu - to eat) 食べて (tabete)
  5. 泳ぐ (oyogu - to swim) 泳いで (oyoide)

Exercise 2: Complete the Sentences

Use the te-form to complete these sentences:

  1. 朝ご飯を食べ＿＿、学校に行きます。
    Asa gohan o tabe___, gakkō ni ikimasu. 食べて (tabete)
  2. ここで写真を撮っ＿＿もいいですか。
    Koko de shashin o tot___ mo ii desu ka. 撮って (totte)
  3. 音楽を聞い＿＿います。
    Ongaku o kii___ imasu. 聞いて (kiite)
  4. 窓を開け＿＿ください。
    Mado o ake___ kudasai. 開けて (akete)
  5. 日本語を勉強し＿＿みました。
    Nihongo o benkyō shi___ mimashita. 勉強して (benkyō shite)

Exercise 3: Translate into Japanese

Translate these sentences into Japanese using the te-form:

  1. I read a book and went to sleep. 本を読んで、寝ました。
    Hon o yonde, nemashita.
  2. Please wait here. ここで待ってください。
    Koko de matte kudasai.
  3. He is watching TV now. 彼は今テレビを見ています。
    Kare wa ima terebi o mite imasu.
  4. May I open the window? 窓を開けてもいいですか。
    Mado o akete mo ii desu ka.
  5. I want to try eating sushi. 寿司を食べてみたいです。
    Sushi o tabete mitai desu.
