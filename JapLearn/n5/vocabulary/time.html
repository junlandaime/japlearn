Japanese Time Expressions

Essential vocabulary for telling time, days, months, and seasons

Introduction to Japanese Time Expressions

Time expressions are essential for everyday communication in Japanese. From telling the current time to making appointments, discussing schedules, or talking about dates and seasons, these expressions form a fundamental part of the language.

In this lesson, we'll cover the vocabulary for days of the week, months, seasons, telling time, and other common temporal expressions used in Japanese.

Cultural Note: The Japanese calendar traditionally follows both the Gregorian calendar and the Imperial calendar system which marks years based on the era of the reigning emperor. The current era is Reiwa (令和), which began on May 1, 2019 when Emperor Naruhito ascended to the throne. The year 2023 is Reiwa 5 (令和5年) in the Japanese calendar.

Days of the Week (曜日 - Yōbi)

The Japanese days of the week all end with 曜日 (yōbi), which means "day of the week." Each day is associated with a celestial body or element.

English Japanese Romaji Associated With Audio
Monday 月曜日 Getsuyōbi Moon (月 - getsu)
Tuesday 火曜日 Kayōbi Fire/Mars (火 - ka)
Wednesday 水曜日 Suiyōbi Water/Mercury (水 - sui)
Thursday 木曜日 Mokuyōbi Wood/Jupiter (木 - moku)
Friday 金曜日 Kin'yōbi Gold/Venus (金 - kin)
Saturday 土曜日 Doyōbi Earth/Saturn (土 - do)
Sunday 日曜日 Nichiyōbi Sun (日 - nichi)

Example Sentences:

今日は月曜日です。

Kyō wa getsuyōbi desu.

Today is Monday.

会議は水曜日にあります。

Kaigi wa suiyōbi ni arimasu.

The meeting is on Wednesday.

日曜日は何をしますか。

Nichiyōbi wa nani o shimasu ka.

What do you do on Sunday?

Putting It Together

To express a time, you simply combine the hour with the minutes. In Japanese, the hour comes first, followed by the minutes.

English Japanese Romaji Audio
1:00 一時 Ichiji
2:30 二時半 or 二時三十分 Niji-han or Niji-sanjuppun
9:15 九時十五分 Kuji jūgofun
12:45 十二時四十五分 Jūniji yonjūgofun
4:20 四時二十分 Yoji nijuppun

Note: The word 半 (han) means "half" and can be used to express half-hour times, such as 1:30 (一時半 - ichiji-han). Also, some numbers change pronunciation when combined with counters, such as 1 minute (いっぷん - ippun) instead of いちふん (ichifun).

AM and PM

In Japanese, "AM" is 午前 (gozen) and "PM" is 午後 (gogo). These terms are placed before the time.

English Japanese Romaji Audio
7:00 AM 午前七時 Gozen shichiji
3:30 PM 午後三時半 Gogo sanji-han
11:15 AM 午前十一時十五分 Gozen jūichiji jūgofun

Example Sentences:

今、何時ですか。

Ima, nanji desu ka.

What time is it now?

午前7時に起きます。

Gozen shichiji ni okimasu.

I wake up at 7:00 AM.

授業は9時から12時までです。

Jugyō wa kuji kara jūniji made desu.

The class is from 9:00 to 12:00.

Seasons (季節 - Kisetsu)

Japan has four distinct seasons, and they play an important role in Japanese culture, art, and daily life.

Spring
春
はる (Haru)

March-May: Cherry blossoms (桜 - sakura) bloom

Summer
夏
なつ (Natsu)

June-August: Hot and humid with many festivals

Autumn/Fall
秋
あき (Aki)

September-November: Colorful foliage (紅葉 - kōyō)

Winter
冬
ふゆ (Fuyu)

December-February: Cold with snow in many regions

Example Sentences:

日本の春はとてもきれいです。

Nihon no haru wa totemo kirei desu.

Spring in Japan is very beautiful.

私の好きな季節は秋です。

Watashi no sukina kisetsu wa aki desu.

My favorite season is autumn.

Other Useful Time Expressions

Here are some common time-related expressions that are frequently used in everyday Japanese conversation.

Japanese Romaji English Example Audio
今日 Kyō Today 今日は忙しいです (Kyō wa isogashii desu) - Today is busy
昨日 Kinō Yesterday 昨日映画を見ました (Kinō eiga o mimashita) - I watched a movie yesterday
明日 Ashita Tomorrow 明日は雨です (Ashita wa ame desu) - Tomorrow will be rainy
今週 Konshū This week 今週はテストがあります (Konshū wa tesuto ga arimasu) - There is a test this week
先週 Senshū Last week 先週京都に行きました (Senshū Kyōto ni ikimashita) - I went to Kyoto last week
来週 Raishū Next week 来週友達に会います (Raishū tomodachi ni aimasu) - I will meet my friend next week
今月 Kongetsu This month 今月は忙しいです (Kongetsu wa isogashii desu) - This month is busy
先月 Sengetsu Last month 先月東京に行きました (Sengetsu Tōkyō ni ikimashita) - I went to Tokyo last month
来月 Raigetsu Next month 来月日本に行きます (Raigetsu Nihon ni ikimasu) - I will go to Japan next month
去年 Kyonen Last year 去年大学を卒業しました (Kyonen daigaku o sotsugyō shimashita) - I graduated from university last year
今年 Kotoshi This year 今年は忙しいです (Kotoshi wa isogashii desu) - This year is busy
来年 Rainen Next year 来年大学に入ります (Rainen daigaku ni hairimasu) - I will enter university next year

Exercise 3: Complete the Dialogue

Fill in the blanks with appropriate time expressions:

A: すみません、＿＿＿＿ですか。

B: ＿＿＿＿です。

A: ありがとうございます。授業は＿＿＿＿からですね。

B: はい、そうです。＿＿＿＿までです。

A: ＿＿＿＿はテストがありますか。

B: いいえ、＿＿＿＿です。

B: 九時です。 (Kuji desu.)

B: はい、そうです。十二時までです。 (Hai, sō desu. Jūniji made desu.)

A: 来週はテストがありますか。 (Raishū wa tesuto ga arimasu ka?)
