How to Form the Volitional

The conjugation rules for the volitional form depend on which verb group the verb belongs to. Let's look at how to form the volitional for each group:

Group 1: U-Verbs (五段動詞・ごだんどうし)

These are verbs whose dictionary form ends in a う-sound (u, ku, gu, su, tsu, nu, bu, mu, ru). To form the volitional:

Change the final う-sound to the corresponding お-sound + う

Dictionary Form Volitional Form Meaning
話す (はなす) 話そう (はなそう) to speak → let's speak
書く (かく) 書こう (かこう) to write → let's write
泳ぐ (およぐ) 泳ごう (およごう) to swim → let's swim
待つ (まつ) 待とう (まとう) to wait → let's wait
読む (よむ) 読もう (よもう) to read → let's read
帰る (かえる) 帰ろう (かえろう) to return → let's return

Group 2: RU-Verbs (一段動詞・いちだんどうし)

These are verbs whose dictionary form ends in いる (iru) or える (eru). To form the volitional:

Remove the final る and add よう

Dictionary Form Volitional Form Meaning
食べる (たべる) 食べよう (たべよう) to eat → let's eat
見る (みる) 見よう (みよう) to see → let's see
起きる (おきる) 起きよう (おきよう) to wake up → let's wake up
出る (でる) 出よう (でよう) to exit → let's exit

Group 3: Irregular Verbs (不規則動詞・ふきそくどうし)

There are only two irregular verbs in Japanese, and they have special volitional forms:

Dictionary Form Volitional Form Meaning
する (する) しよう (しよう) to do → let's do
来る (くる) 来よう (こよう) to come → let's come

Polite Volitional Form

The volitional form also has a polite version, which is formed by adding ましょう (mashou) to the verb stem:

Plain Volitional

  • 話そう (hanasou) - let's speak
  • 食べよう (tabeyou) - let's eat
  • しよう (shiyou) - let's do

Polite Volitional

  • 話しましょう (hanashimashou) - let's speak
  • 食べましょう (tabemashou) - let's eat
  • しましょう (shimashou) - let's do

Note: The polite form ～ましょう is more commonly used when making invitations or suggestions to others, especially in formal situations or with people you don't know well. The plain form ～よう is more casual and often used when expressing your own intentions or with close friends.

Negative Volitional

Unlike many other verb forms, the volitional form does not have a direct negative form. To express "let's not do something," Japanese uses a different structure:

Plain Negative Suggestion

verb + のはやめよう

verb + no wa yameyou

Let's not (do something)

Polite Negative Suggestion

verb + のはやめましょう

verb + no wa yamemashou

Let's not (do something)

Example:

今日は外に出るのはやめよう。

Kyou wa soto ni deru no wa yameyou.

Let's not go outside today.

Example:

お酒を飲むのはやめましょう。

Osake o nomu no wa yamemashou.

Let's not drink alcohol.
