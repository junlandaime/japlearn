Practice Exercises

Test your understanding of the potential form with these practice exercises.

Exercise 1: Conjugate to the Potential Form

Convert each verb to its potential form.

1. 行く (いく) - to go

行ける (いける)

Ikeru - can go

2. 買う (かう) - to buy

買える (かえる)

Kaeru - can buy

3. 聞く (きく) - to listen/hear

聞ける (きける)

Kikeru - can hear/listen

4. 食べる (たべる) - to eat

食べられる (たべられる)

Taberareru - can eat

Colloquial: 食べれる (たべれる) - Tabereru

5. する - to do

できる

Dekiru - can do

Exercise 2: Transform Sentences

Change each sentence to use the potential form.

1. 私は日本語を話します。

Watashi wa nihongo o hanashimasu.

I speak Japanese.

私は日本語が話せます。

Watashi wa nihongo ga hanasemasu.

I can speak Japanese.

2. 彼女は車を運転します。

Kanojo wa kuruma o untenshimasu.

She drives a car.

彼女は車が運転できます。

Kanojo wa kuruma ga unten dekimasu.

She can drive a car.

3. 私たちはここで泳ぎます。

Watashitachi wa koko de oyogimasu.

We swim here.

私たちはここで泳げます。

Watashitachi wa koko de oyogemasu.

We can swim here.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Using the wrong particle

❌ 私は日本語を話せます。

Watashi wa nihongo o hanasemasu.

✓ 私は日本語が話せます。

Watashi wa nihongo ga hanasemasu.

When using the potential form, the object particle typically changes from を (o) to が (ga). However, in casual speech, many Japanese people still use を (o), so you might hear both patterns.

Mistake 2: Confusing Group 1 and Group 2 conjugations

❌ 話る (はなる)

Hanaru

✓ 話せる (はなせる)

Hanaseru

For Group 1 verbs, you must change the final う-sound to the corresponding え-sound and add る. Don't just add る to the stem.

Mistake 3: Forgetting that する has a special potential form

❌ される

Sareru

✓ できる

Dekiru

Remember that する (suru) has a completely different word for its potential form: できる (dekiru).

Summary

Key Takeaways

  • • The potential form expresses ability ("can do") or possibility.
  • • Group 1 (U) verbs: Change the final う-sound to the corresponding え-sound + る (話す → 話せる).
  • • Group 2 (RU) verbs: Remove る and add られる (食べる → 食べられる).
  • • Irregular verbs: する → できる, 来る → 来られる.
  • • The object particle often changes from を (o) to が (ga) with the potential form.
  • • The potential form conjugates like a regular Group 2 verb.
