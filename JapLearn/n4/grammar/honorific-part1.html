Introduction to Honorific Language (Keigo)

Honorific language, or 敬語 (keigo), is a crucial aspect of Japanese communication that reflects Japan's hierarchical society and emphasis on respect and politeness. Keigo is used to show respect to the person you're talking to or about, based on their social status, age, or your relationship with them.

At the N4 level, you'll need to understand the basic concepts and forms of keigo, which will help you navigate social situations appropriately in Japanese.

JLPT N4 Point: While mastering keigo requires years of practice, the JLPT N4 exam expects you to recognize basic honorific forms and understand when they should be used. This includes recognizing common honorific verbs and understanding the difference between the three main types of keigo.

There are three main types of keigo:

1. Sonkeigo (尊敬語)

Respectful language used to elevate the status of the person you're talking about.

2. Kenjougo (謙譲語)

Humble language used to lower your own status or the status of your in-group.

3. Teineigo (丁寧語)

Polite language used in general conversation, typically ending with です/ます.

In this lesson, we'll explore each type of keigo, learn how to form them, and understand when to use them in different situations.

Teineigo (丁寧語) - Polite Language

Teineigo is the most basic form of polite language in Japanese. You've likely been using it since you started learning Japanese, as it's the standard form taught to beginners.

Characteristics of Teineigo

  • Uses です for nouns and adjectives
  • Uses ます form for verbs
  • Uses polite vocabulary choices
  • Used in general conversations with people you don't know well, in business settings, or when speaking formally

Casual Form:

これは本だ。

Kore wa hon da.

This is a book.

Teineigo:

これは本です。

Kore wa hon desu.

This is a book.

Casual Form:

明日、学校に行く。

Ashita, gakkō ni iku.

I will go to school tomorrow.

Teineigo:

明日、学校に行きます。

Ashita, gakkō ni ikimasu.

I will go to school tomorrow.

Polite Vocabulary in Teineigo

Beyond using です and ます, teineigo also involves using more polite vocabulary choices:

Casual Polite Meaning
今 ただいま Now
はい/うん はい Yes
いいえ/ううん いいえ No
さようなら 失礼します Goodbye
ごめん すみません Sorry
