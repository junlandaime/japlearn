Comparing Prohibition Forms

Let's compare the different prohibition forms to understand when to use each one:

Form Formality Level Typical Usage Example
〜ないでください Polite Standard polite prohibition; suitable for most situations ここで写真を撮らないでください。
Please don't take photos here.
〜ではいけません Formal Rules, regulations, strong prohibitions この建物内で喫煙してはいけません。
Smoking is not allowed inside this building.
〜ないほうがいいです Polite advisory Giving advice, suggesting against something その道は危ないから、行かないほうがいいですよ。
That road is dangerous, so it's better not to go there.
〜ないで Casual Among friends and family そんなに心配しないで。
Don't worry so much.
〜てはだめ / 〜ちゃだめ Casual to semi-polite Casual situations, can be used with です for more politeness ここで遊んじゃだめ。
You shouldn't play here.
〜な Very casual / Rough Emergency situations, between very close friends, in fiction 動くな！
Don't move!

Cultural Note: In Japanese culture, direct prohibitions can sometimes be seen as confrontational. This is why there are so many different ways to express prohibition, ranging from very direct to quite indirect. The more indirect forms (like 〜ないほうがいいです) are often preferred in situations where maintaining harmony is important.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Match the Situation with the Appropriate Prohibition Form

Match each situation with the most appropriate prohibition form.

1. A sign in a museum

A. 触るな！

2. A doctor giving advice to a patient

B. お酒を飲まないほうがいいですよ。

3. A school rule

C. 校内で走ってはいけません。

4. A mother to her child

D. そこで遊んじゃだめよ。

5. A police officer in an emergency

E. 写真を撮らないでください。

Answers:

  1. E. 写真を撮らないでください。(Please don't take photos.)
  2. B. お酒を飲まないほうがいいですよ。(It would be better not to drink alcohol.)
  3. C. 校内で走ってはいけません。(Running is not allowed inside the school.)
  4. D. そこで遊んじゃだめよ。(You shouldn't play there.)
  5. A. 触るな！(Don't touch!)

Exercise 2: Transform Sentences into Prohibition Forms

Transform the following sentences into the requested prohibition form.

1. 教室で食べる (〜ないでください form)

2. ここに車を止める (〜てはいけません form)

3. 遅く寝る (〜ないほうがいいです form)

4. そんなに心配する (〜ないで form)

5. この部屋に入る (〜ちゃだめ form)

Answers:

  1. 教室で食べないでください。
    Kyoushitsu de tabenaide kudasai.
    Please don't eat in the classroom.
  2. ここに車を止めてはいけません。
    Koko ni kuruma o tomete wa ikemasen.
    You must not park your car here.
  3. 遅く寝ないほうがいいです。
    Osoku nenai hou ga ii desu.
    It's better not to go to bed late.
  4. そんなに心配しないで。
    Sonna ni shinpai shinaide.
    Don't worry so much.
  5. この部屋に入っちゃだめ。
    Kono heya ni haicha dame.
    You shouldn't enter this room.

Exercise 3: Choose the Appropriate Prohibition Form

For each situation, choose the most appropriate prohibition form from the options provided.

1. You are a teacher telling students not to talk during an exam.

a. 話すな！

b. 話さないで。

c. 話さないでください。

d. 話してはいけません。

2. You are telling your friend not to worry about a small problem.

a. 心配しないでください。

b. 心配してはいけません。

c. 心配しないで。

d. 心配するな！

3. A doctor is advising a patient with high blood pressure.

a. 塩分を取るな！

b. 塩分を取らないほうがいいですよ。

c. 塩分を取っちゃだめ。

d. 塩分を取らないで。

4. A sign in a library.

a. 静かにしないで。

b. 静かにしないでください。

c. うるさくしてはいけません。

d. うるさくするな！

5. A mother to her young child who is about to touch something dangerous.

a. 触らないでください。

b. 触ってはいけません。

c. 触っちゃだめよ！

d. 触らないほうがいいですよ。

Answers:

  1. d. 話してはいけません。(Talking is not allowed.)
  2. c. 心配しないで。(Don't worry.)
  3. b. 塩分を取らないほうがいいですよ。(It would be better not to consume salt.)
  4. c. うるさくしてはいけません。(Making noise is not allowed.)
  5. c. 触っちゃだめよ！(Don't touch it!)

Explanations:

  1. In a formal classroom setting, the teacher would use a formal prohibition form.
  2. Between friends, a casual form is most natural.
  3. A doctor would typically give advice in a polite but not commanding way.
  4. Public signs typically use formal prohibition forms.
  5. A mother would use a casual but firm prohibition with her child.

Common Mistakes

Using the Wrong Level of Formality

Incorrect:

（先生に）宿題を忘れるな！

(To a teacher) Shukudai o wasureru na!

Correct:

（先生に）宿題を忘れないでください。

(To a teacher) Shukudai o wasurenaide kudasai.

Using the 〜な form with someone of higher status or in a formal situation is inappropriate and can be considered rude. Always match the level of formality to the social context.

Incorrect Formation of the ないでください Form

Incorrect:

食べないください。

Tabenai kudasai.

Correct:

食べないでください。

Tabenaide kudasai.

Don't forget to include the particle で between the ない form and ください. Without it, the sentence is grammatically incorrect.

Confusing てはいけません with ていけません

Incorrect:

ここで泳いでいけません。

Koko de oyoide ikemasen.

Correct:

ここで泳いではいけません。

Koko de oyoide wa ikemasen.

The particle は is essential in the てはいけません construction. Without it, the meaning changes completely. 泳いでいけません could be interpreted as "I cannot go by swimming," which is very different from "Swimming is not allowed."

Using the Wrong Form with ほうがいい

Incorrect:

遅くまで起きるほうがいいです。

Osoku made okiru hou ga ii desu.

(When trying to say "It's better not to stay up late.")

Correct:

遅くまで起きないほうがいいです。

Osoku made okinai hou ga ii desu.

When using ほうがいい to express prohibition, you must use the negative form of the verb (ない form). Using the affirmative form changes the meaning to a recommendation to do something rather than not to do it.

Overusing the Plain Prohibition Form (〜な)

Many learners, especially those influenced by anime or manga, tend to overuse the plain prohibition form 〜な in situations where it would be considered rude or inappropriate.

Remember: The 〜な form is appropriate only in very specific contexts, such as:

  • Emergency situations
  • Between very close male friends (in some cases)
  • When expressing strong emotions
  • In certain fictional contexts

In most everyday situations, use one of the more polite forms of prohibition.

Summary

In this lesson, we've learned about the various ways to express prohibition in Japanese:

  • Polite Forms:
    • 〜ないでください - Standard polite prohibition
    • 〜てはいけません / 〜ではいけません - Formal prohibition
    • 〜ないほうがいいです - Polite advisory prohibition
  • Casual Forms:
    • 〜ないで - Casual prohibition
    • 〜てはだめ / 〜ちゃだめ - Casual to semi-polite prohibition
    • 〜な - Plain, direct prohibition (use with caution)

Remember to choose the appropriate form based on the social context, your relationship with the listener, and the level of formality required. Japanese culture places great importance on politeness and social harmony, so using the right level of formality in your prohibitions is essential for effective communication.
