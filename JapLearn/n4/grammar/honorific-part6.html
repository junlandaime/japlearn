Practice and Exercises

Let's practice using keigo in different situations. Try to identify which type of keigo (sonkeigo, kenjougo, or teineigo) would be appropriate in each scenario, and then transform the given sentences accordingly.

Exercise 1: Identifying Keigo Types

For each situation, identify which type of keigo would be most appropriate.

Situation 1:

You are talking to your boss about what your colleague did yesterday.

Teineigo (for talking to your boss) + Sonkeigo (for talking about your colleague)

Situation 2:

You are talking to a customer about what you will do for them.

Teineigo (for talking politely) + Kenjougo (for talking about yourself to a customer)

Situation 3:

You are talking to your friend about what your teacher said in class.

Casual form (for talking to a friend) + Sonkeigo (for talking about your teacher)

Situation 4:

You are talking to your teacher about what you did for homework.

Teineigo (for talking politely) + Kenjougo (for talking about yourself to your teacher)

Exercise 2: Transforming Sentences

Transform the following sentences into the appropriate keigo form based on the given situation.

Sentence 1:

山田さんは今、本を読んでいます。

Yamada-san wa ima, hon o yonde imasu.

Situation: You are talking to your colleague about your boss (Yamada-san).

山田さんは今、本をお読みになっています。

Yamada-san wa ima, hon o o-yomi ni natte imasu.

(Using sonkeigo to show respect to your boss)

Sentence 2:

私は明日、先生に会います。

Watashi wa ashita, sensei ni aimasu.

Situation: You are telling your teacher about your plan to meet them tomorrow.

私は明日、先生にお目にかかります。

Watashi wa ashita, sensei ni o-me ni kakarimasu.

(Using kenjougo to humble yourself when talking to your teacher)

Sentence 3:

社長は何を食べましたか？

Shachō wa nani o tabemashita ka?

Situation: You are asking your colleague what the company president ate.

社長は何を召し上がりましたか？

Shachō wa nani o meshiagarimashita ka?

(Using sonkeigo to show respect to the company president)
