JLPT N5 Exam Overview

Learn about the structure, content, and requirements of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) N5 level.

What is the JLPT N5?

The Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is the largest standardized test of Japanese language proficiency for non-native speakers. The N5 level is the starting point of the JLPT, testing basic Japanese language skills. It measures your ability to understand basic Japanese in everyday situations.

According to the official JLPT website, passing the N5 level certifies that you:

"Can understand some basic Japanese. One is able to read and understand typical expressions and sentences written in hiragana, katakana, and basic kanji. One is also able to listen and comprehend conversations about topics regularly encountered in daily life and classroom situations, and is able to pick up necessary information from short conversations spoken slowly."

Exam Information: The JLPT is held twice a year in most countries (July and December). Registration typically opens 3-4 months before the exam date. Visit the official JLPT website for specific dates and registration information in your country.

JLPT N5 Exam Structure

The JLPT N5 exam consists of two main sections: "Language Knowledge (Vocabulary/Grammar) & Reading" and "Listening." Here's a breakdown of each section:

Language Knowledge & Reading

Duration: 105 minutes

Total Points: 120 points

This section tests:

  • Vocabulary (basic words and phrases)
  • Grammar (basic sentence structures)
  • Reading comprehension (short passages)

Listening

Duration: Approximately 40 minutes

Total Points: 60 points

This section tests:

  • Ability to understand basic conversations
  • Picking out key information from short dialogues
  • Comprehending simple instructions and statements

Passing Score

To pass the JLPT N5, you need to:

  • Score above the minimum required score in each section (typically around 38% for each section)
  • Achieve a total score of at least 80 points out of the total 180 points (approximately 44%)

Note that the exact passing scores can vary slightly from year to year. The JLPT uses a scaled scoring system, so the raw points might be adjusted.

What to Expect on the JLPT N5

Vocabulary

  • Approximately 800 basic words
  • Common everyday vocabulary
  • Basic adjectives and verbs
  • Essential expressions

Grammar

  • Basic sentence structures
  • Essential particles (は, が, を, に, で)
  • Simple tenses (present, past)
  • Basic question forms

Kanji

  • Approximately 100 basic kanji
  • Common kanji used in everyday life
  • Simple compound words
  • Basic kanji readings

Example Question Types

Vocabulary Section:

  • Choose the correct reading for a kanji word
  • Select the appropriate word to complete a sentence
  • Match pictures with the correct vocabulary

Grammar Section:

  • Select the correct particle to complete a sentence
  • Choose the correct word order
  • Fill in blanks in conversations with appropriate responses

Reading Section:

  • Read short passages and answer comprehension questions
  • Understand simple instructions or notices
  • Interpret basic information from schedules or advertisements

Listening Section:

  • Listen to short dialogues and select the appropriate picture
  • Answer questions about conversations
  • Choose the correct response to a spoken statement
