N5 Listening Practice

Improve your Japanese listening skills with these JLPT N5 level exercises.

Introduction to Listening Practice

Listening is one of the four key language skills tested in the JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test). At the N5 level, you'll need to be able to understand basic conversations, simple instructions, and short announcements in Japanese.

This page offers a variety of listening exercises that mimic the format of the JLPT N5 test. Each exercise includes an audio clip followed by questions to test your comprehension. The exercises gradually increase in difficulty to help you build your skills.

Practice Tip: When practicing listening, focus on understanding the main idea first, then gradually work on catching specific details. Don't worry if you don't understand every word - this is normal and will improve with practice.

How to Use This Page:

  1. Listen to the audio clip (you can replay it as many times as needed)
  2. Answer the questions based on what you heard
  3. Check your answers by clicking the "Check Answers" button
  4. Review the explanations for any questions you got wrong
  5. Move on to the next exercise when you're ready

Exercise 1: Simple Greetings

In this exercise, you'll hear short greetings and need to identify the appropriate responses or situations. Listen carefully to the audio and select the best answer for each question.

Listening Exercise 1

Audio duration: 01:24

Question 1

What time of day is the greeting "おはようございます" (Ohayō gozaimasu) typically used?

Correct Answer: A. Morning

Explanation: "おはようございます" is the standard Japanese morning greeting, equivalent to "Good morning" in English. It's typically used from when you wake up until around 10 or 11 AM.

Question 2

You hear: "すみません、トイレはどこですか？" What is the person asking for?

Correct Answer: B. The location of the bathroom

Explanation: "トイレはどこですか" means "Where is the bathroom?" The person is asking for directions to the bathroom/toilet.

Question 3

You hear: "いらっしゃいませ" (Irasshaimase). Where are you most likely to hear this?

Correct Answer: C. At a shop or restaurant

Explanation: "いらっしゃいませ" is a formal welcome phrase used in shops, restaurants, and other businesses when customers enter. It's equivalent to "Welcome" or "May I help you?"
