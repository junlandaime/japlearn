Practice Exercises

Test your understanding of the 〜たら conditional form with these practice exercises.

Exercise 1: Conjugate to the 〜たら Form

Convert each word to its 〜たら conditional form.

1. 行く (いく) - to go

行ったら (いったら)

Ittara - if/when (I/you/etc.) go

2. 食べる (たべる) - to eat

食べたら (たべたら)

Tabetara - if/when (I/you/etc.) eat

3. 高い (たかい) - expensive

高かったら (たかかったら)

Takakattara - if (it) is expensive

4. 静か (しずか) - quiet

静かだったら (しずかだったら)

Shizuka dattara - if (it) is quiet

5. する - to do

したら (したら)

Shitara - if/when (I/you/etc.) do

Exercise 2: Convert to Negative 〜たら Form

Change each word to its negative 〜たら conditional form.

1. 行く (いく) - to go

行かなかったら (いかなかったら)

Ikanakattara - if (I/you/etc.) don't go

2. 高い (たかい) - expensive

高くなかったら (たかくなかったら)

Takakunakattara - if (it) is not expensive

3. 学生 (がくせい) - student

学生じゃなかったら (がくせいじゃなかったら)

Gakusei janakattara - if (he/she) is not a student

Exercise 3: Identify the Usage

Determine whether each sentence expresses a temporal sequence, specific condition, or discovery.

1. 家に帰ったら、誰もいませんでした。

Ie ni kaettara, dare mo imasen deshita.

Discovery

This sentence expresses an unexpected discovery that was made when the speaker got home.

2. 食事が終わったら、映画を見ましょう。

Shokuji ga owattara, eiga o mimashou.

Temporal Sequence

This sentence expresses a sequence of events: first the meal will end, then we will watch a movie.

3. 明日雨が降ったら、傘を持って行きます。

Ashita ame ga futtara, kasa o motte ikimasu.

Specific Condition

This sentence expresses a specific condition: if it rains tomorrow (and only tomorrow), I'll take an umbrella.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Confusing 〜たら with 〜ば for general rules

❌ 毎日運動したら、健康になります。

Mainichi undou shitara, kenkou ni narimasu.

✓ 毎日運動すれば、健康になります。

Mainichi undou sureba, kenkou ni narimasu.

For general rules or principles that always apply, the 〜ば form is more appropriate than 〜たら.

Mistake 2: Using 〜たら for commands

❌ ここに来たら。

Koko ni kitara.

✓ ここに来てください。

Koko ni kite kudasai.

The 〜たら form cannot be used by itself as a command. Use the appropriate command forms instead.

Mistake 3: Using 〜たら with the wrong tense in the main clause

❌ 明日、雨が降ったら、試合は中止でした。

Ashita, ame ga futtara, shiai wa chuushi deshita.

✓ 明日、雨が降ったら、試合は中止です。

Ashita, ame ga futtara, shiai wa chuushi desu.

When talking about future conditions, use the present tense in the main clause, not the past tense.

Summary

Key Takeaways

  • • The 〜たら conditional form is used to express temporal sequences, specific conditions, discoveries, and hypothetical situations.
  • • It is formed by adding ら to the past tense form of verbs, adjectives, and nouns with the copula.
  • • The 〜たら form is more versatile than the 〜ば form and can be used in a wider range of situations.
  • • It is particularly useful for expressing a sequence of events ("when" or "after") and specific, one-time conditions.
  • • The 〜たら form is one of four conditional forms in Japanese, each with its own nuances and usage patterns.
