Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Transform Verbs into Causative Form

Transform the following verbs into their causative form.

1. 飲む (nomu)

2. 帰る (kaeru)

3. 立つ (tatsu)

4. 勉強する (benkyō suru)

5. 食べる (taberu)

Answers:

  1. 飲ませる (nomaseru)
    to make/let (someone) drink
  2. 帰らせる (kaeraseru)
    to make/let (someone) go home
  3. 立たせる (tataseru)
    to make/let (someone) stand
  4. 勉強させる (benkyō saseru)
    to make/let (someone) study
  5. 食べさせる (tabesaseru)
    to make/let (someone) eat

Exercise 2: Complete the Sentences

Complete the following sentences using the causative form of the verbs in parentheses.

1. 先生は生徒に本を＿＿＿＿。(読む)

2. 母は子供を早く＿＿＿＿。(寝る)

3. 上司は部下に残業を＿＿＿＿。(する)

4. 父は息子にピアノを＿＿＿＿。(弾く)

5. コーチは選手を毎日＿＿＿＿。(走る)

Answers:

  1. 先生は生徒に本を読ませました。
    Sensei wa seito ni hon o yomasemashita.
    The teacher made/let the students read a book.
  2. 母は子供を早く寝させました。
    Haha wa kodomo o hayaku nesasemashita.
    The mother made/let the child go to bed early.
  3. 上司は部下に残業をさせました。
    Jōshi wa buka ni zangyō o sasemashita.
    The boss made the subordinates work overtime.
  4. 父は息子にピアノを弾かせました。
    Chichi wa musuko ni piano o hikasemashita.
    The father made/let his son play the piano.
  5. コーチは選手を毎日走らせました。
    Kōchi wa senshu o mainichi hashirasemashita.
    The coach made the athletes run every day.

Exercise 3: Choose the Correct Particle

Choose the correct particle (に or を) to complete each sentence.

1. 先生は学生（　）教室を掃除させました。

a. に

b. を

2. 母は子供（　）早く寝させました。

a. に

b. を

3. 上司は部下（　）報告書を書かせました。

a. に

b. を

4. 先生は生徒（　）立たせました。

a. に

b. を

5. 医者は患者（　）薬を飲ませました。

a. に

b. を

Answers:

  1. a. に (The students are cleaning the classroom - transitive verb with object)
  2. b. を (The child is sleeping - intransitive verb)
  3. a. に (The subordinates are writing a report - transitive verb with object)
  4. b. を (The students are standing - intransitive verb)
  5. a. に (The patients are taking medicine - transitive verb with object)

Explanation:

  • Use に when the verb is transitive (takes an object)
  • Use を when the verb is intransitive (doesn't take an object)

Common Mistakes

Incorrect Formation of Causative

Incorrect:

食べるさせる (taberu saseru)

Correct:

食べさせる (tabesaseru)

For Group 2 verbs (ichidan verbs), you must remove the final る before adding させる. Don't simply add させる to the dictionary form.

Using the Wrong Particle

Incorrect:

先生は生徒を本を読ませました。

Sensei wa seito o hon o yomasemashita.

Correct:

先生は生徒に本を読ませました。

Sensei wa seito ni hon o yomasemashita.

When using a transitive verb (a verb that takes an object) in the causative form, the person being made to do something takes the に particle, not the を particle. This is because the sentence already has an object marked with を.

Confusing Causative with Potential Form

Incorrect Usage:

子供を泳がせる。(When trying to say "The child can swim.")

Correct:

子供は泳げる。

Kodomo wa oyogeru.

The child can swim. (Potential form)

The causative form (〜させる) means "to make/let someone do something," while the potential form (〜える/〜られる) means "can do something." Be careful not to confuse these two forms.

Incorrect Causative Form of Irregular Verbs

Incorrect:

するさせる (suru saseru)

Correct:

させる (saseru)

The irregular verbs する (suru) and 来る (kuru) have special causative forms: させる (saseru) and 来させる (kosaseru). Remember these special forms.

Misinterpreting the Meaning

The causative form can mean either "to make someone do something" or "to let someone do something" depending on the context. Misinterpreting this meaning can lead to confusion.

Tip: To clarify whether you mean "make" or "let," you can use additional context or specific expressions:

  • 無理に〜させる (muri ni ~saseru) - to force someone to do something
  • 自由に〜させる (jiyū ni ~saseru) - to let someone freely do something
