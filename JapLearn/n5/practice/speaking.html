N5 Speaking Practice

Improve your Japanese speaking skills with interactive practice exercises.

Introduction to Speaking Practice

Speaking is an essential skill for mastering Japanese. At the N5 level, you'll need to be able to hold basic conversations, introduce yourself, ask and answer simple questions, and navigate everyday situations.

This page offers a variety of speaking exercises that mimic real-life conversations. Each exercise includes a dialogue with audio pronunciation guides to help you practice your speaking skills.

Speaking Tip: Practice speaking aloud, even if you're alone. Listen to the audio samples and try to mimic the pronunciation, rhythm, and intonation. Recording yourself and comparing with the original can also be very helpful.

How to Use This Page:

  1. Read through the dialogue
  2. Listen to the audio samples for correct pronunciation
  3. Practice speaking the lines out loud
  4. Try to memorize common phrases and expressions
  5. Use the role-play exercises to practice in different situations

Exercise 1: Basic Greetings

Let's start with some basic greetings. Practice these common expressions that you'll use in everyday conversations.

Dialogue: Meeting Someone New

こんにちは。

Konnichiwa.

Hello.

こんにちは。はじめまして。

Konnichiwa. Hajimemashite.

Hello. Nice to meet you.

私は田中です。あなたは？

Watashi wa Tanaka desu. Anata wa?

I am Tanaka. And you?

私は山田です。どうぞよろしく。

Watashi wa Yamada desu. Dōzo yoroshiku.

I am Yamada. Nice to meet you (Please treat me well).

Pronunciation Tips:

  • In "こんにちは (Konnichiwa)", the "chi" sound is softer than in English, more like "konnichiwa".
  • For "はじめまして (Hajimemashite)", emphasize the "ma" slightly: "hajimemashite".
  • The "u" in "です (desu)" is often very soft or almost silent, so it sounds more like "des".
  • For "よろしく (yoroshiku)", there's a slight roll to the "r" sound, somewhere between an English "r" and "l".

Practice Exercise:

Try practicing this dialogue with a friend, or by yourself playing both roles. Focus on proper pronunciation and natural flow.

You can also try substituting your own name in place of "Yamada" or using different greetings like:

  • おはようございます (Ohayō gozaimasu) - Good morning
  • こんばんは (Konbanwa) - Good evening

Exercise 2: Asking and Answering Questions

Now let's practice asking and answering basic questions in Japanese. These question patterns are essential for everyday conversations.

Dialogue: Getting to Know Someone

出身はどこですか？

Shusshin wa doko desu ka?

Where are you from?

アメリカ出身です。田中さんは？

Amerika shusshin desu. Tanaka-san wa?

I'm from America. And you, Tanaka-san?

私は東京出身です。

Watashi wa Tokyo shusshin desu.

I'm from Tokyo.

何歳ですか？

Nan-sai desu ka?

How old are you?

25歳です。趣味は何ですか？

Nijū-go-sai desu. Shumi wa nan desu ka?

I'm 25 years old. What are your hobbies?

音楽を聴くことと、映画を見ることです。

Ongaku o kiku koto to, eiga o miru koto desu.

Listening to music and watching movies.

Pronunciation Tips:

  • Question particles like "か (ka)" should have a slight rising intonation at the end.
  • In "どこ (doko)", both syllables are pronounced with equal stress.
  • The "r" in "アメリカ (Amerika)" is pronounced more like a flap, similar to the "tt" in "butter" in American English.
  • For numbers like "25歳 (nijū-go-sai)", there is a slight pause between "nijū" and "go".

Practice Exercise:

Try asking and answering these questions with your own information. Practice with the following question patterns:

  • 〜は何ですか？ (~ wa nan desu ka?) - What is ~?
  • 〜はどこですか？ (~ wa doko desu ka?) - Where is ~?
  • 〜はいつですか？ (~ wa itsu desu ka?) - When is ~?
  • 〜はだれですか？ (~ wa dare desu ka?) - Who is ~?

Exercise 3: Role Play Scenarios

Practice your speaking skills with these common real-life scenarios. Try to act them out as naturally as possible.

At a Restaurant

Practice ordering food and drinks at a restaurant.

Key Phrases:

  • メニューをください。 - Menu o kudasai. (Please give me the menu.)
  • これをお願いします。 - Kore o onegaishimasu. (I'll have this, please.)
  • 水をください。 - Mizu o kudasai. (Water, please.)
  • おいしいです。 - Oishii desu. (It's delicious.)
  • お会計をお願いします。 - O-kaikei o onegaishimasu. (The bill, please.)

Try this scenario with a friend or practice both roles yourself!

Asking for Directions

Practice asking for and giving directions to a location.

Key Phrases:

  • 〜はどこですか？ - ~ wa doko desu ka? (Where is ~?)
  • まっすぐ行ってください。 - Massugu itte kudasai. (Please go straight.)
  • 右に曲がってください。 - Migi ni magatte kudasai. (Please turn right.)
  • 左に曲がってください。 - Hidari ni magatte kudasai. (Please turn left.)
  • ありがとうございます。 - Arigatō gozaimasu. (Thank you.)

Try asking for directions to a station, store, or other location!

Remember, speaking practice is most effective when done regularly. Try to practice these scenarios at least a few times a week!
