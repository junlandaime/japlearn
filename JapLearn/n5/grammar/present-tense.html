Present Tense

Expressing current and habitual actions in Japanese

Introduction to the Present Tense in Japanese

In Japanese, what we call the "present tense" is actually used to express both present and future actions. This form is also known as the "non-past tense" because it covers any action that isn't in the past.

The present tense in Japanese is used to express:

  • Current actions or states (I eat, I am eating)
  • Habitual actions (I eat breakfast every day)
  • Future actions (I will eat dinner later)
  • General truths (Water boils at 100°C)

Note: Unlike English, Japanese doesn't distinguish between simple present (I eat) and present continuous (I am eating). The context usually makes it clear which meaning is intended.

Plain Present Tense Form

The plain form of present tense (also called dictionary form) is used in casual conversations with friends and family, in written Japanese, and in subordinate clauses.

For Verbs

The plain present form of verbs is their dictionary form:

Group 1 (Godan) Verbs

End with any of the う-row kana: う, つ, る, く, ぐ, す, ぬ, ぶ, む

Examples: 話す (hanasu - to speak), 飲む (nomu - to drink)

Group 2 (Ichidan) Verbs

End with る preceded by an い or え sound

Examples: 食べる (taberu - to eat), 見る (miru - to see)

Group 3 (Irregular) Verbs

Just two verbs: する (suru - to do) and 来る (kuru - to come)

Plus their compounds, like 勉強する (benkyō suru - to study)

For Adjectives

Adjectives also have plain present forms:

い-Adjectives

Used as is, with the い ending

Examples: 高い (takai - expensive/high), 大きい (ōkii - big)

な-Adjectives

Add だ after the adjective

Examples: 静かだ (shizuka da - quiet), 綺麗だ (kirei da - beautiful)

For Nouns

When a noun functions as the predicate, add だ after the noun:

私は学生だ。

Watashi wa gakusei da.

I am a student.

Examples of Plain Present Tense

私は毎日ご飯を食べる。

Watashi wa mainichi gohan o taberu.

I eat rice every day.

友達は今映画を見る。

Tomodachi wa ima eiga o miru.

My friend is watching a movie now.

彼は日本語を話す。

Kare wa nihongo o hanasu.

He speaks Japanese.

このりんごは大きい。

Kono ringo wa ōkii.

This apple is big.

あの部屋は静かだ。

Ano heya wa shizuka da.

That room is quiet.

Polite Present Tense Form (〜ます Form)

The polite form of present tense is used in formal situations, with people you don't know well, with superiors, or in business settings.

For Verbs

To form the polite present tense, you need to convert the verb to its ます (masu) form:

Verb Type How to Form Dictionary Form Polite Form
Group 1 (Godan) Change the last kana from う-row to い-row + ます 話す (hanasu) 話します (hanashimasu)
Group 1 (Godan) Change the last kana from う-row to い-row + ます 買う (kau) 買います (kaimasu)
Group 2 (Ichidan) Remove final る + ます 食べる (taberu) 食べます (tabemasu)
Group 2 (Ichidan) Remove final る + ます 見る (miru) 見ます (mimasu)
Group 3 (する) Change する to します する (suru) します (shimasu)
Group 3 (来る) Change 来る to 来ます 来る (kuru) 来ます (kimasu)

For Adjectives

Adjectives also have polite present forms:

Adjective Type How to Form Plain Form Polite Form
い-Adjectives Keep the い ending + です 高い (takai) 高いです (takai desu)
な-Adjectives Add です (no だ) 静かだ (shizuka da) 静かです (shizuka desu)

For Nouns

For nouns, replace だ with です:

私は学生です。

Watashi wa gakusei desu.

I am a student.

Examples of Polite Present Tense

私は毎日ご飯を食べます。

Watashi wa mainichi gohan o tabemasu.

I eat rice every day.

友達は今映画を見ます。

Tomodachi wa ima eiga o mimasu.

My friend is watching a movie now.

彼は日本語を話します。

Kare wa nihongo o hanashimasu.

He speaks Japanese.

このりんごは大きいです。

Kono ringo wa ōkii desu.

This apple is big.

あの部屋は静かです。

Ano heya wa shizuka desu.

That room is quiet.

Using the Present Tense

1. Current Actions or States

The present tense can describe actions that are happening now:

今、私は本を読みます。

Ima, watashi wa hon o yomimasu.

I am reading a book now.

2. Habitual Actions

The present tense is used for actions that happen regularly:

私は毎朝コーヒーを飲みます。

Watashi wa maiasa kōhī o nomimasu.

I drink coffee every morning.

3. Future Actions

The present tense can also express future actions, often with time words:

明日、友達に会います。

Ashita, tomodachi ni aimasu.

I will meet my friend tomorrow.

4. General Truths

The present tense is used for facts that are always true:

太陽は東から昇ります。

Taiyō wa higashi kara noborimasu.

The sun rises from the east.

5. Ability or Potential

The present tense with potential form can express ability:

彼は日本語が話せます。

Kare wa nihongo ga hanasemasu.

He can speak Japanese.

Note: The potential form (話せる, 食べられる, etc.) will be covered in more detail in a separate lesson.

Common Time Expressions with Present Tense

Here are some time expressions that are frequently used with the present tense:

Japanese Romaji English Example
今 ima Now 今、勉強します。 (Ima, benkyō shimasu.)
I am studying now.
毎日 mainichi Every day 毎日、走ります。 (Mainichi, hashirimasu.)
I run every day.
毎朝 maiasa Every morning 毎朝、新聞を読みます。 (Maiasa, shinbun o yomimasu.)
I read the newspaper every morning.
毎週 maishū Every week 毎週、映画を見ます。 (Maishū, eiga o mimasu.)
I watch a movie every week.
毎月 maigetsu/maitsuki Every month 毎月、友達に会います。 (Maigetsu, tomodachi ni aimasu.)
I meet my friend every month.
毎年 maitoshi/mainan Every year 毎年、日本に行きます。 (Maitoshi, Nihon ni ikimasu.)
I go to Japan every year.
いつも itsumo Always いつも、ここで昼ご飯を食べます。 (Itsumo, koko de hiru gohan o tabemasu.)
I always eat lunch here.
時々 tokidoki Sometimes 時々、映画館に行きます。 (Tokidoki, eigakan ni ikimasu.)
I sometimes go to the movie theater.
明日 ashita Tomorrow 明日、試験があります。 (Ashita, shiken ga arimasu.)
There is an exam tomorrow.
来週 raishū Next week 来週、京都に行きます。 (Raishū, Kyōto ni ikimasu.)
I will go to Kyoto next week.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Convert to Polite Form

Convert these plain present tense verbs to their polite form:

  1. 飲む (nomu - to drink)
  2. 食べる (taberu - to eat)
  3. 行く (iku - to go)
  4. する (suru - to do)
  5. 見る (miru - to see)

Answers:

  1. 飲みます (nomimasu)
  2. 食べます (tabemasu)
  3. 行きます (ikimasu)
  4. します (shimasu)
  5. 見ます (mimasu)

Exercise 2: Complete the Sentences

Fill in the blanks with the appropriate present tense form:

  1. 私は毎日日本語を勉強___________。(する - polite form)
  2. 彼は水を___________。(飲む - plain form)
  3. 明日、友達と映画を___________。(見る - polite form)
  4. この本は___________。(面白い - polite form)
  5. あの部屋は___________。(静か - plain form)

Answers:

  1. 私は毎日日本語を勉強します。(Watashi wa mainichi nihongo o benkyō shimasu.)
  2. 彼は水を飲む。(Kare wa mizu o nomu.)
  3. 明日、友達と映画を見ます。(Ashita, tomodachi to eiga o mimasu.)
  4. この本は面白いです。(Kono hon wa omoshiroi desu.)
  5. あの部屋は静かだ。(Ano heya wa shizuka da.)
