Introduction to Animal Vocabulary

Animal vocabulary (動物・どうぶつ・dōbutsu) is an essential part of learning any language. In Japanese, animal names are often descriptive and can offer insights into how these creatures are perceived in Japanese culture. Learning animal vocabulary will help you describe the world around you and participate in everyday conversations.

For the JLPT N5 level, you'll focus on common animals that you're likely to encounter in daily conversation, including pets, farm animals, and some wild animals.

Cultural Note: Animals play significant roles in Japanese culture and folklore. For example, foxes (狐・きつね・kitsune) are considered magical creatures with shapeshifting abilities, while cats (猫・ねこ・neko) are often associated with good fortune.

(I have a dog.)

(That cat is black.)

(Birds are flying in the sky.)

(She has two hamsters.)

(The rabbit is white.)

(Turtles walk slowly.)
金魚 (きんぎょ) Kingyo Goldfish 金魚は水槽の中で泳いでいます。
(The goldfish is swimming in the tank.)

Example Dialogue:

A: ペットを飼っていますか。

A: Petto o katte imasu ka?

A: Do you have any pets?

B: はい、猫を二匹飼っています。

B: Hai, neko o nihiki katte imasu.

B: Yes, I have two cats.

A: いいですね。私は犬が好きです。

A: Ii desu ne. Watashi wa inu ga suki desu.

A: That's nice. I like dogs.

Grammar Note: When counting animals in Japanese, you use special counters called 助数詞 (じょすうし - josūshi). For most small animals, you use 匹 (ひき - hiki). For example, one dog is 犬一匹 (いぬいっぴき - inu ippiki), two cats is 猫二匹 (ねこにひき - neko nihiki).

Farm Animals

Farm animals are also important vocabulary for everyday conversations.

Japanese Romaji English Example Sentence Audio
牛 (うし) Ushi Cow 牛はミルクを出します。
(Cows produce milk.)
馬 (うま) Uma Horse 彼は馬に乗るのが好きです。
(He likes to ride horses.)
豚 (ぶた) Buta Pig 豚は泥の中で遊びます。
(Pigs play in mud.)
羊 (ひつじ) Hitsuji Sheep 羊はフワフワしています。
(Sheep are fluffy.)
山羊 (やぎ) Yagi Goat 山羊はチーズを作るのに使います。
(Goats are used to make cheese.)
鶏 (にわとり) Niwatori Chicken 鶏は卵を産みます。
(Chickens lay eggs.)
アヒル Ahiru Duck アヒルは池で泳いでいます。
(Ducks are swimming in the pond.)

Example Sentences:

農場に牛と馬がいます。

Nōjō ni ushi to uma ga imasu.

There are cows and horses on the farm.

子供たちは羊の毛で遊んでいます。

Kodomotachi wa hitsuji no ke de asonde imasu.

The children are playing with sheep's wool.

Wild Animals

Here are some common wild animals that you may need to know in Japanese.

Japanese Romaji English Example Sentence Audio
象 (ぞう) Zō Elephant 象は大きな耳を持っています。
(Elephants have big ears.)
虎 (とら) Tora Tiger 虎は縞模様があります。
(Tigers have stripes.)
獅子 (しし) Shishi Lion 獅子は動物の王様です。
(The lion is the king of animals.)
キリン Kirin Giraffe キリンは首が長いです。
(Giraffes have long necks.)
猿 (さる) Saru Monkey 猿は木に登ります。
(Monkeys climb trees.)
狼 (おおかみ) Ōkami Wolf 狼は月に向かって吠えます。
(Wolves howl at the moon.)
熊 (くま) Kuma Bear 熊は冬に眠ります。
(Bears sleep in winter.)
鹿 (しか) Shika Deer 鹿は森に住んでいます。
(Deer live in forests.)
狐 (きつね) Kitsune Fox 日本の昔話には狐が出てきます。
(Foxes appear in Japanese folklore.)
パンダ Panda Panda パンダは竹を食べます。
(Pandas eat bamboo.)

Example Dialogue:

A: 好きな動物は何ですか。

A: Suki na dōbutsu wa nan desu ka?

A: What's your favorite animal?

B: 虎が好きです。かっこいいと思います。あなたは？

B: Tora ga suki desu. Kakkoii to omoimasu. Anata wa?

B: I like tigers. I think they're cool. How about you?

Cultural Note: In Japanese culture and mythology, certain animals have symbolic meanings. For example, foxes (狐・きつね) are considered clever and sometimes magical creatures, while the crane (鶴・つる) represents longevity and good fortune.

Example Sentences:

夏には蝉の声が聞こえます。

Natsu ni wa semi no koe ga kikoemasu.

In summer, you can hear the cicadas' sounds.

庭に蝶がたくさんいます。

Niwa ni chō ga takusan imasu.

There are many butterflies in the garden.
