Introduction to the Causative Form

The causative form (使役形, shieki-kei) in Japanese is used to express that someone makes or lets someone else do something. It's a way to show that one person causes another person to perform an action.

There are two main meanings of the causative form:

1. Making someone do something

This usage implies that the subject is forcing, ordering, or compelling someone to do something. It can range from a direct command to a gentle encouragement.

2. Letting someone do something

This usage implies that the subject is allowing or permitting someone to do something. It indicates giving permission or not preventing an action.

JLPT N4 Point: The causative form is an important grammar point for the JLPT N4 level. You should be able to recognize and use the causative form with different verb types and understand its various nuances.

Let's look at some basic examples of the causative form:

先生は学生に宿題をさせました。

Sensei wa gakusei ni shukudai o sasemashita.

The teacher made the students do homework.

母は子供にテレビを見させました。

Haha wa kodomo ni terebi o misasemashita.

The mother let the child watch TV.

In this lesson, we'll learn how to form the causative for different types of verbs, how to use it in sentences, and the various nuances it can express.

How to Form the Causative

The causative form is created differently depending on the type of verb. Let's look at how to form the causative for each verb group:

Group 1 Verbs (Godan Verbs)

For Group 1 verbs (godan verbs), replace the final う sound with あ sound + せる.

う → あ + せる

Dictionary Form Causative Form Meaning
話す (hanasu) 話させる (hanasaseru) to make/let (someone) speak
待つ (matsu) 待たせる (mataseru) to make/let (someone) wait
読む (yomu) 読ませる (yomaseru) to make/let (someone) read
買う (kau) 買わせる (kawaseru) to make/let (someone) buy
泳ぐ (oyogu) 泳がせる (oyogaseru) to make/let (someone) swim
死ぬ (shinu) 死なせる (shinaseru) to let (someone) die
遊ぶ (asobu) 遊ばせる (asobaseru) to let (someone) play

Group 2 Verbs (Ichidan Verbs)

For Group 2 verbs (ichidan verbs), remove the final る and add させる.

る → させる

Dictionary Form Causative Form Meaning
食べる (taberu) 食べさせる (tabesaseru) to make/let (someone) eat
見る (miru) 見させる (misaseru) to make/let (someone) see/watch
起きる (okiru) 起きさせる (okisaseru) to make/let (someone) wake up
寝る (neru) 寝させる (nesaseru) to make/let (someone) sleep
着る (kiru) 着させる (kisaseru) to make/let (someone) wear

Irregular Verbs

The two irregular verbs in Japanese have special causative forms:

Dictionary Form Causative Form Meaning
する (suru) させる (saseru) to make/let (someone) do
来る (kuru) 来させる (kosaseru) to make/let (someone) come

Note: For 来る (kuru), the alternative causative form 来させる (kosaseru) is also used, though 来させる (kosaseru) is more common in modern Japanese.

Causative Sentence Structure

When using the causative form, the sentence structure changes to accommodate the person being made or allowed to do something. Let's look at the basic patterns:

Basic Pattern

Person A は Person B に/を Verb (causative form)

This pattern means "Person A makes/lets Person B do something." The choice between に (ni) and を (o) particles for Person B depends on the verb type:

に (ni) Particle

Used with transitive verbs (verbs that take an object). In this case, the object takes the を (o) particle.

を (o) Particle

Used with intransitive verbs (verbs that don't take an object).

先生は学生に本を読ませました。

Sensei wa gakusei ni hon o yomasemashita.

The teacher made the students read a book.

(読む is transitive, so 学生 takes に)

母は子供を公園で遊ばせました。

Haha wa kodomo o kouen de asobasemashita.

The mother let the child play in the park.

(遊ぶ is intransitive, so 子供 takes を)

Common Patterns and Expressions

Here are some common patterns and expressions using the causative form:

1. Making someone do something (obligation)

先生は生徒に宿題をたくさんさせました。

Sensei wa seito ni shukudai o takusan sasemashita.

The teacher made the students do a lot of homework.

2. Letting someone do something (permission)

親は子供に好きな食べ物を選ばせました。

Oya wa kodomo ni suki na tabemono o erabasemashita.

The parents let the child choose their favorite food.

3. Causative + てあげる/てくれる (doing something for someone)

先生は生徒に休ませてあげました。

Sensei wa seito ni yasumasete agemashita.

The teacher let the students rest (did them a favor).

4. Causative + てもらう (getting someone to do something)

先生に作文を読ませてもらいました。

Sensei ni sakubun o yomasete moraimashita.

I got the teacher to read my composition.
