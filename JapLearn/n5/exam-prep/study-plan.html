JLPT N5 Study Plan

A comprehensive 12-week study plan to prepare for the JLPT N5 exam.

Your JLPT N5 Study Plan

This 12-week study plan is designed to help you prepare efficiently for the JLPT N5 exam. The plan is divided into three phases, each focusing on different aspects of your preparation.

Study Tip: Consistency is key! It's better to study for 30 minutes every day than to cram for 5 hours once a week. Adjust this plan to fit your schedule, but try to maintain consistency.

Phase 1: Fundamentals

Weeks 1-4

  • Master hiragana and katakana
  • Learn basic vocabulary (100-200 words)
  • Study essential grammar patterns
  • Practice basic kanji (30-50 characters)

Phase 2: Building Skills

Weeks 5-8

  • Expand vocabulary (400-500 words)
  • Learn intermediate grammar patterns
  • Study more kanji (50-80 characters)
  • Practice reading short passages
  • Begin listening practice

Phase 3: Test Preparation

Weeks 9-12

  • Complete N5 vocabulary list (800 words)
  • Review all grammar patterns
  • Master all N5 kanji (100 characters)
  • Take practice tests regularly
  • Focus on weak areas

Weekly Study Schedule

Track Your Progress

Monitoring your progress is crucial for effective study. Here are some ways to track your JLPT N5 preparation:

Checklist Method

Create checklists for each study area:

  • • Vocabulary list (mark words you've learned)
  • • Grammar points checklist
  • • Kanji learned and mastered
  • • Practice tests completed

Progress Graph

Track your practice test scores over time:

  • • Take a practice test every 2-3 weeks
  • • Chart your scores for each section
  • • Note improvement areas
  • • Adjust study focus based on results

Study Journal

Keep a daily study journal:

  • • Record what you studied each day
  • • Note difficulties encountered
  • • Write what worked well
  • • Plan for the next day's session
