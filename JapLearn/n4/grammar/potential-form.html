Potential Form (～できる)

Expressing ability and possibility in Japanese

Understanding the Potential Form

N4 Level

The potential form (可能形・かのうけい) in Japanese is used to express the ability or possibility to do something. It's similar to "can" or "be able to" in English. This grammar point is essential for expressing your capabilities or discussing whether something is possible.

Key Point: In Japanese, the potential form changes depending on the verb group (Group 1, 2, or 3). Each group follows different conjugation rules, which we'll explore in detail below.

There are two main ways to express potential in Japanese:

1. Verb Potential Form

Most verbs can be conjugated into their potential form by following specific rules based on the verb group.

Example:

日本語を話せます。

Nihongo o hanasemasu.

I can speak Japanese.

2. Using できる (dekiru)

For some situations, especially with nouns and certain verbs, you can use the verb できる (dekiru) which means "can do" or "is possible."

Example:

ピアノが弾けます。

Piano ga hikemasu.

I can play the piano.
